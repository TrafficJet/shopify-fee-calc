# Shopify Payment Processing Fees 2026: Complete Breakdown by Plan

Every dollar you collect through your Shopify store gets touched by payment processing fees before it reaches your bank account. Knowing exactly what you'll pay—and how to pay less—can save you hundreds or thousands of dollars each year. This guide breaks down the exact rates for every Shopify plan in 2026, with real-dollar math so you can pick the right plan for your sales volume.

## What Are Shopify Payment Processing Fees?

Two distinct fee types apply to every Shopify store, and confusing them is one of the most common (and costly) mistakes merchants make. **Credit card processing fees** are charged on every sale regardless of which payment gateway you use—these cover the cost of actually running the card. **Shopify's additional transaction fee** is a separate charge that Shopify applies only when you use a third-party gateway instead of Shopify Payments.

When you enable Shopify Payments, Shopify waives the additional transaction fee entirely. You still pay credit card processing rates, but you eliminate that second layer of cost. PCI compliance, fraud analysis tools, and chargeback management are bundled into Shopify Payments at no extra charge (Shopify, 2026).

Your actual effective rate depends on your plan tier, whether the sale happens online or in person via Shopify POS, and the card type used. Accelerated checkouts like Shop Pay, Apple Pay, and Google Pay don't add any surcharge beyond the standard card rate.

## Shopify Basic Plan Fees: Best for Stores Under ~$22K/Month

On the Basic plan at $39/month, your online credit card rate through Shopify Payments is **2.9% + $0.30** per transaction. In-person sales processed through Shopify POS come in lower at **2.6% + $0.10**. If you use a third-party gateway instead of Shopify Payments, Shopify adds a **2.0%** surcharge on top of whatever your gateway charges (Shopify, 2026).

Here's the math on $10,000/month in online sales (assuming an average order value of $50, so 200 transactions):

- **With Shopify Payments:** ($10,000 × 2.9%) + (200 × $0.30) = $290 + $60 = **$350 total**
- **With a third-party gateway:** You pay the gateway's own rate *plus* ($10,000 × 2.0%) = **$200 additional** to Shopify

That $200 difference makes the choice straightforward for most sellers. The Basic plan typically suits stores doing under roughly $22,000/month in gross merchandise volume (GMV)—the point at which upgrading to Standard starts saving money on processing fees alone.

Merchants who migrate from marketplace selling to their own storefront often start here. A small handmade jewelry shop moving off Etsy to a standalone Shopify store, for example, would keep fixed costs low on Basic while testing direct-to-consumer demand.

## Shopify Standard Plan Fees: The Break-Even Math at $22K/Month

The Standard plan costs $105/month and drops your online card rate to **2.6% + $0.30** per transaction with Shopify Payments. In-person rates decrease to **2.5% + $0.10**, and the third-party gateway surcharge falls to **1.0%** (Shopify, 2026).

The key question: when does upgrading from Basic ($39/month) to Standard ($105/month) actually save you money? The monthly cost difference is $66. The online rate difference is 0.3% (2.9% vs. 2.6%). Divide $66 by 0.003, and you get **$22,000/month in GMV** as the break-even point—meaning if your monthly sales consistently exceed roughly $22,000, the Standard plan costs you less overall.

If you're using a third-party gateway like PayPal or Stripe exclusively, the surcharge drops from 2.0% to 1.0%—a full percentage point. At just $6,600/month in GMV, that 1.0% savings alone covers the $66 plan difference. This makes the Standard plan particularly valuable for merchants who have business reasons to stick with a [third-party gateway](/best-payment-gateways-for-shopify).

## Shopify Advanced Plan Fees: Worth It for Features Before Fee Savings

The Advanced plan at $399/month brings your online rate down to **2.4% + $0.30** and your in-person rate to **2.4% + $0.10**. The third-party gateway surcharge drops to just **0.5%** (Shopify, 2026).

Here's what Advanced saves on $50,000/month in online sales (assuming 500 transactions at $100 AOV):

- **Standard plan:** ($50,000 × 2.6%) + (500 × $0.30) + $105 = $1,300 + $150 + $105 = **$1,555/month**
- **Advanced plan:** ($50,000 × 2.4%) + (500 × $0.30) + $399 = $1,200 + $150 + $399 = **$1,749/month**

At $50,000/month, the Standard plan still wins by about $194. The break-even point between Standard and Advanced lands around **$147,000/month in GMV** when you factor in the $294 monthly cost difference divided by the 0.2% rate gap.

The Advanced plan also unlocks third-party calculated shipping rates, advanced analytics, and up to 15 staff accounts—features that deliver value beyond raw fee savings. See our full [Shopify plan comparison](/shopify-plan-comparison) for a feature-by-feature breakdown.

Consider a mid-size DTC skincare brand doing $80,000/month: they might choose Advanced for the analytics and shipping tools even before hitting the pure fee break-even. Merchants who upgrade for features rather than fees alone tend to find value in the custom reporting capabilities, which can reveal margin insights that more than offset the subscription premium.

## Shopify Plus Payment Fees: Negotiable Rates for High-Volume Stores

Shopify Plus starts at **$2,300/month** and uses a variable pricing model that can scale with your revenue at higher tiers. The biggest advantage for payment processing: your Shopify Payments card rate is **negotiable**, typically landing around **2.15% + $0.30** or lower depending on your monthly volume and average order value (Shopify Plus, 2026).

The third-party gateway surcharge on Plus is just **0.15%**—the lowest available. Plus merchants can also accept payments in multiple currencies natively through Shopify Payments, avoiding the friction (and added costs) of currency conversion apps.

Plus gives you access to Checkout Extensibility for custom checkout experiences, which doesn't change your processing rate but can meaningfully improve conversion rates. Your Merchant Success Manager can renegotiate rates as your volume grows past agreed thresholds.

The target merchant for Plus is typically doing **$500K+ in annual GMV**, where even a few tenths of a percentage point in rate reduction translates to thousands in annual savings. One limitation to note: Plus contracts typically require a three-year commitment, so the flexibility to downgrade is limited compared to standard plans. ## Third-Party Gateway Fees: The Stacking Cost Most Merchants Overlook

When you use any payment gateway other than Shopify Payments, Shopify charges an **additional transaction fee on top of whatever that gateway already charges you**. This is Shopify's platform fee for processing the order through external infrastructure, and it stacks directly.

Here's how that math plays out on the Basic plan using Stripe:

- **Stripe's rate:** 2.9% + $0.30
- **Shopify's additional fee:** 2.0%
- **Your effective rate:** 4.9% + $0.30 per transaction

On $10,000 in monthly sales, that's roughly **$520 in total processing costs** compared to approximately $350 with Shopify Payments alone—a $170/month difference that compounds over the year into more than $2,000.

Common third-party gateway base rates for US merchants as of 2026:

- **Stripe:** 2.9% + $0.30 (Stripe, 2026)
- **PayPal:** 2.99% + $0.49 for standard processing (PayPal, 2026)
- **Authorize.net:** 2.9% + $0.30 plus a $25/month gateway fee
- **Square:** 2.9% + $0.30 for online transactions

For most US-based merchants, Shopify Payments is the cheaper path. The exceptions: stores selling [high-risk products](/best-payment-gateways-for-shopify) that Shopify Payments doesn't support (such as CBD or certain supplements), subscription-heavy businesses needing specialized billing platforms like ReCharge with direct gateway access, and merchants in countries where Shopify Payments isn't yet available.

## Shopify Payments: What to Know About Card Types and Chargebacks

All major card brands—Visa, Mastercard, Amex, and Discover—process at the **same advertised rate** through Shopify Payments on your plan. This is a genuine advantage, since Amex historically charges higher interchange rates on other platforms. With Shopify Payments, you pay the same percentage regardless of card brand (Shopify, 2026).

International cards trigger an additional **1.5% currency conversion fee** when the card's currency doesn't match your store's default currency. This applies even if you're using Shopify Payments, so factor this in if you sell to a global customer base. Merchants who see a significant share of international orders—common in niches like vintage collectibles or specialty apparel—should monitor this fee closely in their analytics dashboard.

Chargebacks carry a **$15 fee** per dispute. If you win the dispute, Shopify refunds the $15. Accelerated checkout methods—Shop Pay, Apple Pay, and Google Pay—process at your standard card rate with no added surcharge. For guidance on handling disputes, see our [Shopify chargeback guide](/shopify-chargeback-guide).

## How to Reduce Your Shopify Processing Fees in 2026

**Step 1: Use Shopify Payments.** If you're in a supported country and not in a restricted product category, switching from a third-party gateway to Shopify Payments is the single biggest fee reduction available to you. A $25,000/month apparel store on the Basic plan that switches from Stripe to Shopify Payments eliminates the 2.0% surcharge—saving **$500/month, or $6,000/year**. That's real money that can go straight back to ad spend or inventory.

**Step 2: Upgrade plans at the right time.** Use this formula: **(New plan cost − Current plan cost) ÷ (Current rate − New rate) = Break-even monthly GMV.** Only upgrade when your sales consistently exceed that number for at least two to three consecutive months—seasonal spikes alone don't justify the jump.

**Step 3: Enable Shop Pay.** Shop Pay has been shown to increase checkout conversion by up to 50% compared to guest checkout (Shopify, 2025). Even though the card rate is identical, higher conversion means more revenue per visitor and lower effective customer acquisition cost.

**Step 4: Pay annually.** The ~25% subscription discount on annual billing reduces your fixed costs. On the Standard plan, that's roughly $315 saved per year.

**Step 5: Audit your actual rates.** Go to **Shopify Admin → Analytics → Finances** and review your effective processing rate monthly. If it's higher than your plan's stated rate, investigate international card volume or refund patterns—both can push your effective rate above the published number. For more strategies, visit our guide on [reducing Shopify transaction fees](/reduce-shopify-transaction-fees).

## Shopify Fees vs. Competitors: No Single Platform Is Cheapest for Everyone

**WooCommerce + Stripe** charges no platform transaction fee, but you pay for hosting ($25–$100+/month), SSL certificates, security plugins, and PCI compliance management. For stores under $20,000/month in GMV, these infrastructure costs often equal or exceed the savings from avoiding Shopify's platform fees (WooCommerce, 2026). WooCommerce also requires more hands-on maintenance—plugin updates, security patches, and server optimization are your responsibility.

**BigCommerce** charges zero additional transaction fees on every plan, which is a genuine advantage for merchants using third-party gateways. BigCommerce's recommended payment processors (Braintree, Stripe) charge similar credit card processing rates to Shopify Payments, so the savings are primarily on the platform surcharge layer. See our [Shopify vs. BigCommerce comparison](/shopify-vs-bigcommerce) for the full breakdown.

**Wix eCommerce** charges processing rates through Wix Payments starting at 2.9% + $0.30 on their Business plan (Wix, 2026). Wix doesn't charge additional transaction fees, but its ecosystem of apps and fulfillment integrations is less mature than Shopify's, which can limit growth for merchants scaling past six figures annually.

The honest takeaway: no single platform has the cheapest fees across all scenarios. Shopify's strength is that its bundled fraud tools, Shop Pay's conversion lift, and Shopify Balance for faster payouts create a total cost of ownership that's typically competitive at mid-market volumes ($10K–$500K/month). Merchants who prioritize gateway flexibility without surcharges may find BigCommerce appealing, while those wanting full infrastructure control may prefer WooCommerce despite the added maintenance burden.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Shopify charge a fee if I use PayPal?
Yes. If you use PayPal or any third-party gateway instead of Shopify Payments, Shopify adds an extra transaction fee on top of PayPal's own rate. On Basic that's 2.0%, Standard is 1.0%, Advanced is 0.5%, and Plus is 0.15%.

### What is the cheapest way to accept payments on Shopify?
Enable Shopify Payments to eliminate the additional transaction fee, and choose an annual billing cycle. At higher sales volumes, upgrading to a higher plan reduces your per-transaction rate enough to offset the higher monthly cost. Use the break-even formula in the fee reduction section above to find your optimal plan.

### Are Shopify payment processing fees negotiable?
Only on Shopify Plus. Plus merchants can negotiate lower card rates with Shopify directly once they reach sufficient volume. All other plans have fixed published rates as of 2026.

### Does Shopify charge fees on refunds?
Shopify Payments does not return the Shopify transaction fee on refunds. The credit card processing fee (the percentage portion) is typically returned by the card network, but the fixed $0.30 per-transaction fee is not refunded. This is an important consideration for stores with high return rates—a fashion retailer with a 30% return rate, for example, should factor unrecovered per-transaction fees into margin calculations.

### How does Shopify's 2026 fee structure compare to 2025?
Shopify's core rate structure remained largely stable heading into 2026. The main changes involve Shopify Plus pricing adjustments for high-volume tiers and expanded Shopify Payments availability in additional countries. Always check [Shopify's pricing page](https://www.shopify.com/pricing) for the latest confirmed rates (Shopify, 2026).

### Do I pay credit card fees on Shopify POS in-person sales?
Yes, but in-person rates are lower than online rates. On Basic, in-person is 2.6% + $0.10 versus 2.9% + $0.30 online. You must use Shopify POS with Shopify Payments hardware to access these rates. Check our [Shopify POS hardware review](/shopify-pos-hardware-review) for compatible readers and terminals.
