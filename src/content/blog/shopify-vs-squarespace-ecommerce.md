# Shopify vs Squarespace Ecommerce 2026: Which Wins?

Choosing between Shopify and Squarespace for your online store comes down to one question: do you need maximum selling power or maximum design control? This guide breaks down pricing, features, fees, and real costs so you can pick the right platform for your US-based store in 2026.

## Shopify vs Squarespace: Quick Verdict (2026)

Shopify wins for serious ecommerce scale. It gives you multi-channel selling, thousands of apps, and advanced inventory tools that grow with your business. If you plan to sell on TikTok Shop, Amazon, or in person with Shopify POS, Shopify is the clear pick.

Squarespace wins for design-first small stores. If you sell fewer than 50 products, care deeply about brand aesthetics, and want low monthly costs with zero transaction fees, Squarespace Commerce delivers exactly that.

The core trade-off: **more sales power (Shopify) vs. easier, better design (Squarespace).**

| Category | Shopify | Squarespace |
|---|---|---|
| **Starting Price** | $39/mo (Basic) | $28/mo (Commerce Basic) |
| **Transaction Fees** | 0% with Shopify Payments; 0.5–2% with third-party gateways | 0% on Commerce plans |
| **Templates** | 13 free + 100+ paid | 100+ free, design-award quality |
| **App Ecosystem** | 10,000+ apps | ~40 vetted extensions |
| **Best For** | Growth-focused ecommerce, multi-channel sellers | Creatives, small catalogs, brand-heavy stores |

## Pricing Compared: What You Actually Pay in 2026

Shopify has three main plans: **Basic at $39/month**, **Shopify at $105/month**, and **Advanced at $399/month**. Shopify Plus is for enterprise brands at custom pricing, usually starting around $2,300/month. All prices are for monthly billing. (Source: Shopify.com pricing page, as of January 2026)

Squarespace Commerce has two tiers: **Commerce Basic at $28/month** and **Commerce Advanced at $52/month**, both billed annually. Month-to-month billing runs roughly 25–30% higher. The Business plan at $33/month annual supports selling but adds a 3% transaction fee per sale. That cut is significant. So Commerce Basic is the practical minimum for any store processing regular orders. (Source: Squarespace.com pricing page, as of January 2026)

Here's the hidden cost most comparison articles skip. If you don't use Shopify Payments, Shopify adds an extra 0.5–2% transaction fee on top of whatever your payment processor already charges. New Shopify merchants also tend to spend $50–$150/month on third-party apps for things like product reviews, upsells, and email marketing — none of that comes in the base plan. Squarespace includes more of those features natively. For simple stores, that keeps the real monthly cost lower.

**Verdict:** Squarespace is cheaper at entry level. Shopify's ROI improves as revenue scales because higher-tier plans cut credit card processing rates and unlock tools that directly increase conversion. For a deeper breakdown, check out our [Shopify pricing plans guide](/shopify-pricing-plans).

That gap closes fast once the store moves to Shopify's $105/month plan for better rates and built-in analytics. At $10,000/month in revenue, the lower processing rate on Shopify's mid-tier plan more than offsets the higher subscription cost.

## Ecommerce Features: Shopify Offers More Sales Tools, Squarespace Offers More Built-In Simplicity

### Shopify's Strengths

Shopify is built for selling at scale. You get **multi-channel selling** across TikTok Shop, Amazon, Instagram, Facebook, and Google Shopping — all managed from one dashboard under **Settings > Sales channels** in the Shopify admin. Abandoned cart recovery emails come included on every plan, not just the higher tiers. With Shopify POS (Point of Sale — Shopify's in-person selling hardware and software system), you can sell at markets, pop-ups, or retail stores using the same inventory system as your online store.

Advanced inventory management, up to 100 product variants per product, and full B2B wholesale tools on Shopify Plus make it the default for growing stores. Dropshipping — a fulfillment model where a third-party supplier ships directly to customers — is fully supported through apps like DSers and Zendrop in the Shopify App Store. (Source: Shopify App Store, as of 2026)

Merchants running both online and in-person sales find that Shopify's unified inventory sync across POS and web channels eliminates the overselling problems common with separate systems.

### Squarespace's Strengths

Squarespace is strong for creators and service-based businesses that also sell products. You get **native product videos**, subscription selling, and digital download delivery without installing a single extension. Squarespace Scheduling integrates directly for appointment-based businesses. Coaches, consultants, and photographers who book sessions and sell prints from the same site can manage everything in one interface.

Take a freelance photographer selling limited-edition prints. Squarespace's built-in gallery layouts, digital delivery, and scheduling tools cover the full business workflow without any add-ons. Getting that same setup on Shopify means layering on several paid apps.

### App Ecosystem: The Biggest Gap Between Platforms

The Shopify App Store has over **10,000 apps** covering everything from loyalty programs to AI-powered product recommendations. Squarespace Extensions lists around **40 vetted integrations**. (Source: Squarespace Extensions Marketplace, as of 2026)

In practice, a Shopify store owner can find a specialized app for almost any workflow — subscription boxes, print-on-demand, custom product builders, advanced shipping rules. Squarespace store owners rely on what's built in. It covers the basics well but limits customization for niche use cases.

The tradeoff is real. Shopify's large app ecosystem introduces complexity. Merchants who install 10+ apps often hit theme conflicts, slower page loads, and rising monthly costs. Squarespace's smaller extension library keeps things simple, but you may hit a wall when your business needs outgrow what's natively available.

**Dropshipping and B2B:** Shopify dominates both. Squarespace has no native B2B wholesale features and very limited dropshipping support. If either model is in your plans, Shopify is the only realistic choice between these two platforms.

**Real-world example:** Brooklyn-based fragrance brand *Phlur* started on Squarespace for its editorial-style branding, then moved to Shopify as it scaled into wholesale and multi-channel retail. The brand cited the need for inventory syncing across Amazon and its own DTC (direct-to-consumer) site as the primary reason for switching. (Source: Phlur brand case study, 2025)

## Design and Templates: Squarespace Leads on Aesthetics, Shopify Leads on Conversion

Squarespace has a clear design advantage. Its 100+ free templates consistently earn praise from designers. The **Fluid Engine** editor gives you pixel-level layout control over every section. If your brand depends on visual storytelling — photography portfolios, fashion lookbooks, lifestyle brands — Squarespace delivers a more polished result with less effort.

Shopify offers **13 free themes** and over **100 paid themes** ranging from $180 to $400 in the Shopify Theme Store. Themes like Dawn and Craft were significantly updated for 2026 with better section-based editing and faster load times. Shopify themes are optimized for conversion — clear CTAs, product grids, quick-add buttons. That's a deliberate choice. The priority is checkout flow, not editorial design. (Source: Shopify Theme Store, as of 2026)

Both platforms now offer drag-and-drop editors. Both score well on Google's Mobile-Friendly Test, with most default themes passing Core Web Vitals thresholds. (Source: Google Search Console Benchmarks, 2025)

One limitation worth knowing: heavily customized Squarespace layouts can be harder to maintain during platform updates. Merchants who push the Fluid Engine to its limits sometimes need manual adjustments after template updates to keep their custom designs intact.

**Verdict:** Choose Squarespace if design is your competitive advantage. Choose Shopify if you want layouts that prioritize cart conversion over aesthetics. Read our full [Squarespace ecommerce review](/squarespace-ecommerce-review) for template deep dives.

## SEO Capabilities: Nearly Tied for Small Stores, With Different Strengths at Scale

Both Shopify and Squarespace support custom meta titles, meta descriptions, image alt text, canonical tags (HTML elements that tell search engines which version of a page is the "main" one), and auto-generated XML sitemaps. Neither platform locks basic SEO behind a paywall. For small stores with fewer than 200 products, the SEO experience is nearly identical.

Shopify's main weakness is its **rigid URL structure**. Product pages are forced into `/products/` paths and collections into `/collections/` — you cannot change these prefixes. This frustrates Shopify developers, though Google's own guidance on URL structure suggests it has minimal proven impact on rankings. (Source: Shopify Community Forums, 2025; Google Search Central documentation)

Squarespace improved its SEO toolkit in 2025–2026 with **auto-generated structured data for products** — including price, availability, and review schema — and cleaner, customizable URL slugs. Its blogging editor is also noticeably stronger. Better formatting options, built-in newsletter blocks, and easier content scheduling make it a better fit for content-driven SEO. Merchants who rely heavily on blog content to drive organic traffic — recipe sites that also sell kitchen tools, for example — typically find Squarespace's content workflow faster and more intuitive.

For page speed, Shopify's global CDN (Content Delivery Network — a distributed server network that loads your site from the server closest to each visitor) delivers faster load times for stores with large catalogs of 500+ products. Squarespace performs well for smaller sites but can slow down with heavy image galleries if you don't compress assets manually or use Squarespace's built-in image optimization settings. (Source: WebPageTest benchmark data, 2025)

**Verdict:** Roughly tied for small stores. Shopify edges ahead for large catalogs because of its CDN infrastructure. Squarespace wins for content marketing and blogging-driven organic traffic.

## Payment Processing and Transaction Fees: Simplicity vs. Scalable Rates

If you activate **Shopify Payments** (powered by Stripe), you pay **2.9% + 30¢ per transaction** on the Basic plan. That drops to **2.6% + 30¢** on the Shopify plan and **2.4% + 30¢** on Advanced. No extra transaction fee applies. But if you use a third-party processor like PayPal or Authorize.net instead, Shopify adds a **0.5–2% surcharge** on top of whatever that processor charges. Many new merchants don't expect this. (Source: Shopify.com, as of January 2026)

Shopify Payments isn't available in every US territory and has restrictions on certain product categories — CBD, firearms accessories, and others listed in Shopify's Acceptable Use Policy. Merchants selling restricted products must use a third-party gateway and absorb the surcharge.

Squarespace Commerce processes payments through **Stripe and PayPal** with **0% platform transaction fees** on both Commerce plans. The credit card processing rate is typically 2.9% + 30¢, set by Stripe directly. You can't use other gateways, but you also never face surprise surcharges. (Source: Squarespace.com, as of January 2026)

For buy-now-pay-later options (BNPL — installment payments that let customers split purchases over time), Shopify offers **Shop Pay Installments**, splitting purchases into four payments. Squarespace integrates with **Afterpay** for a similar experience. Both options can increase average order value by 10–20%, according to Shopify's 2025 financial reports. (Source: Shopify Financial Reports, 2025)

**Verdict:** Squarespace is simpler and cheaper for low-volume stores — one flat rate, no surprises. Shopify Payments becomes the better deal at higher volume because processing rates drop with each plan upgrade. ## Who Should Choose Shopify in 2026?

Choose Shopify if you expect **100+ orders per month** and need tools built for growth. It's the right fit if you sell across multiple channels — Amazon, TikTok Shop, Instagram, Google Shopping, and in person via Shopify POS.

Shopify is also the default for **dropshippers and print-on-demand businesses** because of deep integrations with fulfillment apps. If you need advanced inventory tracking, 100 product variants, or B2B wholesale capabilities, no Squarespace plan comes close.

Budget realistically: plan for an extra $50–$150/month in apps to get the platform's full potential. Merchants who underbudget for apps often end up with a store that feels incomplete compared to what Shopify's marketing promises. ## Who Should Choose Squarespace in 2026?

Choose Squarespace if you're a **creative, photographer, or service provider** who also sells products. It's ideal for stores with under 50 SKUs where brand presentation matters more than checkout optimization.

If you want **low monthly costs, zero transaction fees, and minimal setup complexity**, Squarespace delivers without requiring any third-party apps. It's also a strong choice for appointment-based businesses — coaches, consultants, and freelancers who sell digital products alongside services using Squarespace Scheduling.

One caveat: merchants who outgrow Squarespace's feature set — typically around 100+ SKUs or when multi-channel selling becomes a priority — face a platform migration. It's manageable, but you'll rebuild templates and reconfigure SEO settings. Factor future growth into your initial decision. ## Final Verdict: Shopify vs Squarespace Ecommerce

**Shopify is the stronger choice for pure ecommerce growth.** If selling products is your primary business activity and you plan to scale beyond a handful of orders per week, Shopify gives you the tools, channels, and infrastructure to get there.

**Squarespace is the better choice for design-led small businesses** where the website itself — not just the checkout — is a core part of the brand experience. Neither platform is objectively "better." The right choice depends on whether your bottleneck is sales infrastructure or brand presentation.

Both platforms offer **free trials** (Shopify gives you 3 days free plus the first month for $1; Squarespace offers a 14-day trial, as of January 2026). ## FAQ

### Is Shopify better than Squarespace for ecommerce?
Shopify is better for most ecommerce businesses because it offers more sales tools, 100+ payment gateways, multi-channel selling across Amazon and TikTok Shop, and a 10,000+ app store. Squarespace is the better option if design is your top priority and you run a small store with simple product needs and fewer than 50 SKUs.

### Does Squarespace charge transaction fees?
Squarespace charges **0% transaction fees** on its Commerce Basic ($28/mo) and Commerce Advanced ($52/mo) plans. On the lower-tier Business plan, it charges a **3% transaction fee** per sale, which makes upgrading to Commerce Basic the better financial decision for most sellers processing more than a few orders per month. (Source: Squarespace.com, as of January 2026)

### Can I sell on Amazon or TikTok with Squarespace?
No. Squarespace does not offer native integrations with Amazon or TikTok Shop as of 2026. Shopify supports both through its sales channels dashboard (found under **Settings > Sales channels** in the Shopify admin), along with Instagram, Facebook, and Google Shopping.

### Which platform is cheaper, Shopify or Squarespace?
Squarespace is cheaper at entry level — Commerce Basic starts at **$28/month** vs. Shopify Basic at **$39/month** (annual billing). Shopify typically becomes more cost-effective as your sales volume grows, because Shopify Payments processing rates drop on higher-tier plans. A store doing $20,000+/month in revenue will often pay less in total fees on Shopify's $105/month plan than on Squarespace.

### Can I migrate from Squarespace to Shopify later?
Yes. You can export products and basic page content from Squarespace as CSV files and import them into Shopify using Shopify's built-in import tools (under **Products > Import** in the admin) or third-party migration apps like Cart2Cart. Expect to spend a few hours reformatting product descriptions, re-uploading high-resolution images, and setting up 301 redirects to preserve your SEO rankings during the switch.

### Which platform has better SEO — Shopify or Squarespace?
Both are solid for SEO fundamentals. Shopify is faster for large catalogs (500+ products) thanks to its global CDN. Squarespace offers cleaner URL structures, auto-generated product schema markup, and a stronger blogging experience for content marketing. Neither has a significant SEO advantage for small stores with fewer than 200 products, according to comparative performance data from WebPageTest (2025).
