# Best Shopify Oberlo Alternatives in 2025

If you're still searching for Oberlo in 2025, here's what you need to know: it's gone. Shopify officially shut Oberlo down in June 2022, and the app hasn't been available since.

Several alternatives have stepped in to fill the gap — and many of them outperform what Oberlo offered. This guide breaks down the best Shopify Oberlo alternatives so you can pick the right one for your dropshipping store.

---

## Why Oberlo No Longer Works for Shopify Stores

Oberlo was a Shopify-owned app. It let you import products from AliExpress with one click, automate order fulfillment (the process of sending purchased products to customers via a supplier), and sync inventory levels in real time. For years, it was the default tool for Shopify dropshippers.

Shopify discontinued Oberlo on June 15, 2022, and removed it from the App Store entirely (Source: Shopify Help Center, 2025). If you had existing product data and order history in Oberlo, that information was automatically migrated to DSers during the transition period.

The alternatives available now offer more supplier variety, faster US shipping options, and deeper automation than Oberlo provided. Merchants who relied heavily on Oberlo's AliExpress-only pipeline often find that switching to a newer tool actually expands what their store can do.

## DSers: The Official Oberlo Replacement With the Smoothest Migration Path

DSers is Shopify's officially recommended Oberlo successor. It handles AliExpress product sourcing natively. If your workflow relied on Oberlo's AliExpress import-and-fulfill model, DSers replicates that experience almost identically.

**Key features** include bulk ordering (process hundreds of orders in a few clicks), a supplier optimizer that finds cheaper or faster alternatives for existing products, variant mapping (linking your store's product options like size and color to supplier SKUs), and automated tracking updates that push shipping info directly to your Shopify orders.

**Pricing (as of 2025):** DSers offers a free Basic plan that supports up to 3,000 products. The Advanced plan runs ~$19.90/mo and adds 20,000 products plus advanced mapping. The Pro plan costs ~$49.90/mo and includes 75,000 products with priority support (Source: DSers Pricing Page, 2025).

**Real-world example:** A Shopify seller running a pet accessories store migrated 1,200 Oberlo products to DSers using the built-in import tool and resumed fulfillment within 48 hours, according to a case study shared in the DSers community forum. The product-mapping transfer generally goes smoothly. But some sellers report needing to manually re-link suppliers for products that were delisted on AliExpress during the gap.

**Best for:** Store owners who used AliExpress with Oberlo and want the most familiar transition.

**Limitation:** DSers is heavily AliExpress-dependent. US shipping times can stretch to 15–30 days unless you carefully vet suppliers with domestic warehouse options. If fast shipping is a competitive requirement for your niche, this dependency becomes a real constraint.

DSers holds a **4.6-star rating** from over 14,000 reviews on the Shopify App Store (Source: Shopify App Store, 2025).

## AutoDS: Best for Full Automation Across 25+ Suppliers

**Standout features:** An AI-powered product finder scans trending items and shows estimated profit margins. Automated price and stock monitoring adjusts your listings when supplier data changes. Automated fulfillment processes orders without manual intervention, and a print-on-demand integration lets you sell custom products alongside standard inventory.

**Pricing (as of 2025):** Plans start at ~$26.90/mo for the Import 200 plan. AutoDS offers a 14-day trial for $1, making it low-risk to test (Source: AutoDS Pricing Page, 2025).

**What changed in 2025:** AutoDS added TikTok Shop and Facebook Marketplace integrations, letting you sell across multiple channels from one dashboard (Source: AutoDS Blog, 2025). This matters because Statista reported US social commerce revenue hit an estimated $80+ billion in 2024. Sellers who go beyond a single Shopify storefront tend to capture more of that spend.

**Real-world example:** An electronics accessories seller used AutoDS's automated repricing to hold a 32% average profit margin across 400+ SKUs sourced from three different suppliers — without manually checking prices daily. The repricing tool adjusted listings an average of 12 times per week based on supplier cost fluctuations.

**Best for:** Intermediate to advanced sellers who want hands-off order management. If you're processing 50+ orders per day, the automation typically pays for itself quickly.

**Tradeoff:** The learning curve is steeper than DSers. Expect to spend a few hours configuring automation rules before the system runs smoothly. The monthly cost is also higher than free-tier alternatives.

## How to Migrate From Oberlo to a New App

If you had Oberlo data that was already migrated to DSers during the 2022 transition, you can use DSers' export tool to pull your product list and supplier mappings. If you're switching to a different app entirely, follow these steps.

**Step 1:** Export your current product list and order history from your Shopify admin (**Admin → Products → Export**, or from DSers if that's where your Oberlo data landed). Save this as a CSV file for reference.

**Step 2:** Install your chosen replacement app from the Shopify App Store (**Admin → Apps → Search Shopify App Store**) and connect it to your store. Most apps request standard Shopify permissions to read and write products, orders, and fulfillment data.

**Step 3:** Re-map your existing products to new supplier listings within the app. This means matching each product in your store to the correct supplier SKU, including variants like size and color. Budget 1–2 minutes per product for stores with simple catalogs, or longer if you have complex variant structures.

**Step 4:** Run one test order through the full fulfillment cycle before going live. Verify that tracking numbers sync back to Shopify (**Admin → Orders → [Order] → Fulfillment status**) and that the customer receives the correct item.

**Warning:** If you install a new app while another dropshipping app is still active, you risk creating duplicate product listings or conflicting inventory syncs. Deactivate your old app's product sync before connecting the new one.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Oberlo still available for Shopify in 2025?
No. Shopify shut down Oberlo in June 2022. DSers is the official replacement and handles most of the same AliExpress-based dropshipping functions (Source: Shopify Help Center, 2025).

### What is the closest app to Oberlo for Shopify?
DSers is the most direct replacement — it was built to replicate Oberlo's core workflow and even imported existing Oberlo store data during the transition.

### Which Oberlo alternative offers the fastest US shipping?
Zendrop and Spocket both maintain US-based warehouses and typically deliver within 2–7 business days to US customers, making them the top picks for domestic shipping speed.

### Can I use a free Oberlo alternative on Shopify?
Yes. DSers and CJDropshipping both offer free plans. CJDropshipping charges no monthly fee at all — you only pay per order fulfilled. Keep in mind that free plans may have product or order limits.

### Is AutoDS better than DSers?
It depends on your needs. DSers is simpler and free to start, making it a strong fit for beginners using AliExpress. AutoDS supports more suppliers and offers deeper automation, so it typically suits growing stores willing to pay a monthly fee. Neither is objectively "better" — the right choice depends on your store's order volume and supplier requirements.

### Do these apps work with Shopify's current checkout in 2025?
Yes. DSers, AutoDS, Zendrop, Spocket, and CJDropshipping all maintain active Shopify App Store listings and are updated to support Shopify's current checkout and admin architecture (Source: Shopify App Store, 2025).
