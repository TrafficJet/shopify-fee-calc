# How to Improve Print on Demand: 12 Proven Tips

## Why Most Print on Demand Stores Underperform

The US print on demand market is projected to reach $13.2 billion by the end of 2026, growing at roughly 26% year-over-year (Grand View Research, 2026). Despite that growth, most new POD sellers quit within their first six months. The three biggest failure points are weak design quality, choosing the wrong supplier, and publishing listings that never get found in search.

This article walks you through 12 specific, actionable tips to fix each of those problems. Whether you sell on Etsy, Shopify, or Amazon Merch on Demand, these steps apply across every platform and product type.

## 1. Start With High-Resolution, Print-Ready Designs

Every print on demand supplier requires artwork at a minimum of 300 DPI — that's dots per inch, the measure of how many ink dots fit in one printed inch. Anything below 300 DPI will look blurry or pixelated on the final product, whether it's fabric, ceramic, or paper.

Save your files as PNG with a transparent background for apparel and accessories. Use PDF when submitting to suppliers that handle all-over prints or posters. Both **Adobe Illustrator** and **Canva Pro** export at the correct resolution. Canva Pro's background remover also makes transparent PNGs easy to produce.

Never upscale a low-resolution image to hit 300 DPI. A 72 DPI Instagram graphic stretched to 4500×5400 pixels will print with visible pixelation. That leads to customer complaints and 1-star reviews. One Etsy seller documented a 23% drop in return rate after switching all designs from upscaled JPEGs to native 300 DPI PNGs created in Illustrator. Most sellers who skip this step only discover the problem after their first wave of negative reviews — by then, the listing's search ranking has already taken a hit.

## 2. Choose the Right Print on Demand Supplier — Base Cost and Speed Vary Significantly

Your supplier determines print quality, shipping speed, and base cost. All three directly affect your margins and reviews. Here's how three leading suppliers compare for US-based sellers as of 2026:

Domestic US fulfillment centers typically cut delivery time by 2–4 days compared to overseas production. Before you commit, order samples from at least two suppliers. Expect to spend $5–$30 per sample depending on the product.

**Gelato's** sustainability-first approach connects with eco-conscious buyers. That's a real differentiator — 73% of US Gen Z shoppers say they'll pay more for sustainable products (First Insight, 2025). But Printify's lower base costs give you more room to compete on price if your audience cares less about sustainability. The right choice depends on your niche and target customer, not on any single "best" supplier.

## 3. Order Product Samples and Document Everything — Real Photos Outperform Mockups

Sample orders are non-negotiable. You cannot judge print color accuracy, fabric weight, or stitching quality from a digital mockup alone. Order one of every product type you plan to sell — tees, mugs, hoodies, posters — from your chosen supplier.

Photograph each sample in natural light, near a window without harsh shadows. Etsy stores using original photography report conversion rates 18–25% higher than those relying solely on supplier-generated mockups (eRank Seller Insights, 2025). These photos also reduce return rates because customers see exactly what they're getting.

A Shopify POD store selling custom pet portraits saw returns drop from 9% to under 3% after replacing all mockup images with photos of actual printed canvases. The initial cost of samples — typically $50–$150 for a full product line — usually pays for itself within the first few weeks through fewer refund requests and stronger reviews.

## 4. Optimize Your Product Listings for Search — Long-Tail Keywords Drive Qualified Traffic

Strong SEO turns a good design into a product that actually sells. On **Etsy**, put your most important keywords first in the title, fill all 13 tags with specific phrases, and write a keyword-rich description covering materials, sizing, and intended use.

On **Shopify**, keep your meta title under 60 characters, add descriptive alt text to every product image, and clean up your URL slug to include your target keyword. In Shopify admin, go to **Products > [Product] > Search engine listing preview > Edit** to update these fields. Use **Google Trends** and tools like eRank or Marmalead to find niche keywords with decent search volume and low competition.

Target long-tail keywords instead of broad terms. "Funny nurse gift mug 2026" converts far better than "funny mug" because the buyer intent is specific. A seller specializing in healthcare-worker gifts reported that switching from generic to long-tail keywords increased Etsy search impressions by 340% within 60 days. One caveat: long-tail keywords carry lower individual search volume, so you typically need a larger catalog to match the total traffic a broad keyword generates.

## 5. Price Your Products for Profit, Not Just Sales — Target 30–50% Margins

Here's a real margin calculation for a unisex t-shirt sold on Etsy as of 2026:

| Line Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Supplier base cost (Printful) | $12.50 |
| Etsy listing + transaction fees (~11%) | $3.08 |
| Shipping (customer-paid or built into price) | $0.00 |
| Your retail price | $27.99 |
| **Profit per unit** | **$12.41 (44% margin)** |

(Printful pricing + Etsy fee calculator, as of 2026)

Don't race to undercut competitors. That destroys brand value and leaves no room for ad spend. Healthy US POD margins sit between 30–50% after all costs.

You can support premium pricing with high-quality lifestyle mockups, branded packaging inserts like thank-you cards, and discount codes for repeat purchases. Keep in mind that ads eat into these margins. A $27.99 shirt with a $2 ad spend per sale drops your effective margin from 44% to about 37%. Factor ad costs into your pricing from the start.

## 6. Use Trend Tools and AI to Find Winning Niches — Niche Stacking Beats Broad Categories

Finding profitable niches requires a method called niche stacking: combine a specific audience + an occasion + an emotion or humor style. "Dog mom + Mother's Day + sarcastic quote" is far stronger than "dog lover" because it targets a buyer with a clear reason to purchase.

**Google Trends**, Pinterest Trends, and TikTok search are the best free tools for spotting 2026 micro-niches. Search for rising terms, filter by US region, and look for steady upward trajectories — not single-day spikes. Three niches showing strong momentum right now: pickleball retirees, solar eclipse chasers (the 2026 Iceland eclipse is already generating search buzz), and homestead gardening humor.

You can use **Midjourney** or Adobe Firefly to brainstorm design concepts quickly. But treat AI-generated images as rough drafts, not final art. Refine them in Adobe Illustrator or Canva Pro to reach print-ready quality. Also know that copyright protection for purely AI-generated images remains legally uncertain in the US as of 2026 (US Copyright Office, 2023 guidance). Adding substantial human creative input during refinement matters for both legal protection and design quality.

## 7. Improve Mockup Quality to Increase Click-Through Rate — Design for Mobile First

Lifestyle mockups — showing a real person wearing or using your product — typically beat flat-lay images for click-through rate on Etsy and Shopify. Flat lays work best for products like posters, stickers, and phone cases where the design itself is the main visual draw.

Tools like Placeit, Smartmockups, and built-in mockup generators from **Printful** and **Printify** make this accessible without a photo studio. A/B test your thumbnail images: on Etsy, swap the primary listing photo and track views over two weeks. On Shopify, use product page testing apps like Neat A/B Testing to compare variants.

Frame every mockup for mobile first. Over 72% of US online shoppers browse on their phones (Statista, 2026). Your design needs to be legible and eye-catching at thumbnail size on a 6-inch screen. Test listings on an actual phone before publishing — text that looks fine on a desktop monitor can be completely unreadable on mobile, and that kills conversions.

## 8. Streamline Your Production and Fulfillment Workflow — Build in Redundancy for Q4

Connect both **Printify** and **Printful** to your store so you can route orders to a backup supplier if your primary provider experiences delays. This matters most during Q4, when holiday order volume spikes and production times stretch.

Set realistic production and shipping times in your listings. Promising 5-day delivery when your supplier averages 7–10 days during peak season leads to negative reviews and platform penalties. Use Zapier or native Shopify integrations — found under **Settings > Notifications** and your POD app's settings — to sync order data and avoid overselling.

Check supplier outage notices every week. **Printful** and **Printify** both post production status updates on their dashboards. One seller who ignored a 3-day DTG (direct-to-garment) printer maintenance notice during November 2025 ended up with 47 late orders and a temporary Etsy search penalty. Managing two supplier integrations does add complexity — you'll need to keep product catalogs, pricing, and design files synchronized across both. But the insurance against holiday-season delays is usually worth it.

## 9. Collect and Leverage Customer Reviews — Photo Reviews Are Especially Valuable

Send a post-purchase email on day 7 (when the product typically arrives) and again on day 14 (after the customer has had time to use it). On Etsy, a simple thank-you message with a polite review request is the standard approach. On Shopify, apps like **Judge.me** (free tier available) and **Loox** (starting at $9.99/month as of 2026) automate review collection and display photo reviews on product pages.

Stores with a 4.8+ star average tend to rank higher in Etsy's search algorithm (eRank, 2025). Photo reviews are especially valuable — with the customer's permission, repost them as user-generated content on Instagram and TikTok. One **Redbubble** seller who reposted customer photos on TikTok saw a 15% increase in weekly store traffic within a month.

One warning: don't offer discounts or freebies in exchange for positive feedback. Etsy and Amazon both prohibit this. Getting flagged can result in listing removal or account suspension. A genuine, friendly follow-up message is more effective and keeps you compliant.

## 10. Expand to Multiple Sales Channels — Tailor Your Approach to Each Platform's Strengths

Selling on **Etsy**, **Shopify**, **Amazon Merch on Demand**, and **TikTok Shop** at the same time spreads your risk and multiplies your reach. Each channel needs specific adjustments: TikTok Shop favors short video demonstrations, Amazon rewards keyword-dense bullet points, and Etsy prioritizes filled-out tags and attributes.

Vary your product descriptions across channels to avoid duplicate content issues that hurt SEO. Prioritize channels based on where your target audience actually shops — not raw traffic volume. If you sell to Gen Z, TikTok Shop may outperform Amazon. If your niche skews 35+, Etsy and Shopify paid ads often deliver stronger returns.

A POD brand selling pickleball apparel launched on TikTok Shop in early 2026 and generated $4,200 in its first month by posting 15-second styling videos — outselling its Etsy store during the same period. The tradeoff is operational overhead. Managing listings, inventory syncing, customer service, and platform-specific rules across four channels takes significant time or automation tools like LitCommerce or Sellbrite.

## 11. Run Targeted Ads Without Blowing Your Budget — Start Small and Scale on Data

On **Etsy Ads**, start with $1–$3 per day. Only run ads on listings that already have organic sales. Scaling ad spend on unproven listings burns cash with no data to guide you.

For **Shopify** stores, Meta and TikTok retargeting ads typically convert best because you're reaching people who already visited your product page. Pinterest ads work especially well for seasonal and gifting POD products — gift-related searches on Pinterest spike 6–8 weeks before major holidays (Pinterest Business, 2025).

Track ROAS (return on ad spend — revenue generated per dollar spent on ads) by individual product SKU, not your overall store average. This lets you cut losing products fast and reinvest in winners. A healthy ROAS target for POD advertising is 3:1 or higher — $3 in revenue for every $1 spent on ads. Below 2:1, most POD sellers are losing money once supplier costs and platform fees are factored in.

## 12. Analyze Data and Double Down on What Works — Cut Underperformers at 90 Days

Track these KPIs every week: conversion rate, average order value, return rate, and ad ROAS per product. Use Etsy Stats for your Etsy shop, Google Analytics 4 for Shopify, and Shopify Analytics — under **Analytics > Reports** in your admin dashboard — for store-level reporting.

Drop any listing with zero sales after 90 days. A bloated catalog dilutes your focus and makes it harder to spot trends. Instead, double down on proven designs with color variations, product-type extensions (a best-selling mug design adapted to a tote bag, for example), and seasonal versions.

Plan your Q4 calendar early. Designs intended for holiday sales need to be uploaded by mid-September at the latest. That gives them time to index in search and collect early reviews before the November–December rush. Sellers who wait until October often find their new listings buried behind competitors who already have review counts and sales velocity built up.

## FAQ

### What is the most important factor in print on demand success?

Design quality combined with strong SEO listings drives the most results. A great design that nobody finds won't sell. A highly visible listing with a weak design won't convert. Focus on both from day one.

### How do I improve print quality on my POD products?

Use 300 DPI PNG files, order physical samples before listing, and pick a supplier with US-based fulfillment like **Printful** or **Gelato**. Test at least two suppliers to compare output on your specific product types — print quality can vary between product categories even within the same supplier.

### Which print on demand platform is best for quality in 2026?

**Printful** is widely rated highest for consistent print quality and US fulfillment speed as of 2026. **Gelato** is a strong alternative with carbon-neutral shipping. Neither is objectively best for every product type — order samples to verify for your specific designs and materials.

### How long does it take to improve a print on demand store's sales?

Most sellers see meaningful improvement within 60–90 days after optimizing listings, improving mockups, and running Etsy Ads on proven products. Results vary based on niche competitiveness and listing volume. Consistent niche research and new uploads speed up the timeline.

### Should I sell on Etsy or Shopify for print on demand?

Start on **Etsy** for built-in organic traffic, then add **Shopify** once you have revenue to invest in paid ads. Running both channels together in 2026 is the most reliable growth strategy for US POD sellers, though managing two platforms does add operational overhead.

### How do I find trending niches for print on demand?

Use **Google Trends**, Pinterest Trends, and TikTok search to spot rising topics. Look for niche combinations — audience plus occasion plus emotion — rather than broad categories like "dog lover." Validate demand by checking whether similar products on Etsy have recent sales using tools like eRank or Marmalead before investing design time.
