# Shopify Checklist: Launch Your Store Right in 2026

Launching a Shopify store without a plan is like opening a shop with the doors locked. This **Shopify checklist** covers every task you need to complete before, during, and after launch — so your first visitors actually buy something.

---

## Why You Need a Shopify Checklist Before You Launch

Skipping pre-launch steps costs real money. Broken links, missing tax settings, or a checkout that won't accept payments will push your first wave of traffic away — for good. According to Baymard Institute, the average cart abandonment rate sits at 70.19%. Surprise fees and clunky checkout only push that number higher (Baymard Institute, 2025).

Stores that run a full pre-launch audit see measurably higher conversion rates on day one compared to those that launch and fix things later (Shopify Commerce Trends, 2026). This checklist covers **seven key areas**: store setup, design, product pages, payments, shipping and taxes, SEO, and marketing.

It applies whether you're building a brand-new store or relaunching after a rebrand.

**Real-world example:** When DTC skincare brand Dew Mighty relaunched on Shopify, a structured pre-launch audit caught tax configuration errors and broken mobile menu links before going live — saving what could have been thousands in lost first-week sales.

## Store Setup Checklist: Lock In the Foundation Before Touching Design

Your store's foundation determines everything that follows. Get these basics locked in before you touch design or products.

- **Purchase a custom domain.** A `yourbrand.com` address builds trust instantly. The default `yourstore.myshopify.com` subdomain signals "not yet established" to shoppers. You can buy a domain directly through Shopify (typically $14–$20/year for a `.com`) or connect one from a registrar like Cloudflare or Namecheap.
- **Pick the right Shopify plan.** Match your plan to your expected monthly revenue and feature needs. Here's a quick comparison as of 2026:

| Feature | Basic ($39/mo) | Shopify ($105/mo) | Advanced ($399/mo) | Plus (from $2,300/mo) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Staff accounts | 2 | 5 | 15 | Unlimited |
| Online credit card rate | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.7% + 30¢ | 2.5% + 30¢ | Custom |
| Third-party transaction fee | 2.0% | 1.0% | 0.6% | 0.2% |
| Shopify Shipping discount | Up to 77% | Up to 88% | Up to 88% | Custom |
| Duties & import tax | ❌ | ❌ | ✅ | ✅ |

*(Shopify Pricing Page, as of 2026)*

Merchants processing under $10,000/month usually find Basic covers their needs. The savings from lower transaction fees on the Shopify plan only offset the higher monthly cost once you're consistently above that threshold. For a deeper breakdown, read our [Shopify pricing plans comparison](/shopify-pricing-plans).

- **Configure General Settings.** Set your store currency, timezone, and weight units under **Settings → General**. A wrong timezone skews your analytics and order timestamps — most merchants don't notice until they're reconciling sales reports weeks later.

*[Screenshot: Shopify admin General Settings page showing store name, currency, timezone, and unit system fields]*

- **Generate legal pages.** Shopify has built-in generators for Privacy Policy, Terms of Service, Refund Policy, and Shipping Policy. Go to **Settings → Policies** and use the templates as a starting point. Then customize them to match your actual business practices. The generated templates are generic — they may not reflect your specific return windows or data handling. Review them carefully, or talk to a lawyer if you're unsure.
- **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on your admin account. This takes 60 seconds and protects your entire business from unauthorized access.
- **Add staff accounts sparingly.** Use role-based permissions so your VA can't access billing and your fulfillment team can't edit your theme.

## Theme and Design Checklist: Build for Mobile Speed First

Your theme controls how your store looks, feels, and performs. A heavy or poorly coded theme can destroy your mobile conversion rate before you sell a single product.

- **Choose a mobile-first theme.** Start with Shopify's free **Dawn** theme if you're on a budget — it's lightweight, fast, and built on Shopify's Online Store 2.0 architecture. Online Store 2.0 uses a section-based design system that lets you customize every page without touching code. Paid themes from the Shopify Theme Store offer more built-in sections but aren't always faster. Browse our [best Shopify themes roundup](/best-shopify-themes) for specific recommendations.
- **Upload your logo** in SVG or PNG format with a transparent background. Avoid JPEG logos — they lack transparency support and often look blurry on retina screens.
- **Set brand colors and typography** inside the theme editor (**Online Store → Customize → Theme Settings**). Stick to two fonts maximum: one for headings, one for body text. Each additional font file adds to page load time.
- **Test your homepage speed.** Run your URL through Google PageSpeed Insights and target a score of **90+ on mobile**. Anything below 50 means your theme or images need serious work.
- **Keep navigation simple.** Limit your top-level menu to 7 items or fewer. Deep dropdown menus frustrate mobile users and increase bounce rates — especially on touch screens where hover states don't exist.
- **Add a favicon** (the small icon that appears in browser tabs). Go to **Theme Settings → Favicon** and upload a 32×32px version of your logo mark.
- **Check readability on small screens.** Load your store on a 375px-wide screen (iPhone SE size). If body text is hard to read, bump the font size to at least 16px — this also prevents iOS Safari from auto-zooming on form inputs.
- **Verify color contrast.** Use WebAIM's contrast checker to confirm your text-to-background ratio meets WCAG AA standards (a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for body text). This improves both accessibility and readability.

**Real-world example:** Outdoor gear brand Ridge Merino switched from a heavy paid theme to a customized Dawn build. Their mobile PageSpeed score jumped from 38 to 94, and mobile conversion rate went up 12% in the following quarter.

## Product Page Checklist: Turn Browsers Into Buyers

Your product pages do the selling. Every field you leave empty is a missed chance to answer a buyer's question — or rank in Google.

- **Write keyword-rich product titles.** Put your primary keyword near the front. "Organic Cotton Crew Neck T-Shirt — Navy" beats "Navy T-Shirt" every time for search visibility.
- **Structure product descriptions** to answer three questions: *What is it? Who is it for? Why buy it here?* Use short paragraphs and bullet points. Don't copy manufacturer descriptions word-for-word — duplicate content hurts your SEO because Google may filter out pages with text it has already indexed from another domain.
- **Upload 3–5 images per product** minimum. Include a lifestyle shot (product in use), a detail shot (texture, stitching), and a scale reference (product next to a common object or on a model with height listed). For photography tips, check our [ecommerce product photography guide](/ecommerce-product-photography-tips).
- **Add alt text to every image.** Describe what's in the photo: "Woman wearing navy organic cotton t-shirt, front view." Alt text helps visually impaired shoppers using screen readers and gives Google context for image search rankings.
- **Use Compare At Price when running sales.** Setting the original price in this field displays a strikethrough price next to the sale price, showing customers exactly how much they save. Find this field in the **Pricing** section of each product's admin page.
- **Fill in product metafields** for size guides, materials, care instructions, or specs. Metafields are custom data fields that store structured product information beyond the standard title/description/price. They help Shopify themes display information consistently and can feed into rich search results.
- **Install a reviews app.** Social proof directly affects purchase decisions — Spiegel Research Center found that displaying reviews can increase conversion rates by up to 270% for higher-priced products (Spiegel Research Center, Northwestern University, 2017). Apps like **Judge.me** or **Loox** from the Shopify App Store connect directly to your product pages. Our [recommended apps for new stores list](/shopify-apps-for-new-stores) covers the best options.
- **Turn on inventory tracking** under each product variant so out-of-stock items get flagged automatically. Nothing kills trust faster than accepting an order you can't fulfill.

## Payment and Checkout Checklist: Remove Every Reason Not to Buy

A checkout that confuses or surprises your customer is where sales go to die. Complete every item here before your store goes live.

- **Enable Shopify Payments.** This is required for US merchants who want to avoid the extra 0.5%–2.0% third-party transaction fee. Shopify Payments processes Visa, Mastercard, Amex, and Discover. One limitation: Shopify Payments isn't available for every product category — high-risk industries like CBD, firearms accessories, or certain supplements may need a third-party processor. Learn more in our [Shopify Payments setup guide](/shopify-payments-setup).
- **Add backup payment methods.** Activate **Shop Pay**, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and PayPal. Shop Pay has a 91% higher mobile conversion rate compared to standard guest checkout (Shopify, 2026). Different customer segments have strong payment preferences — some shoppers will abandon checkout entirely if their preferred method isn't available.
- **Run a test order.** Use Shopify's **Bogus Gateway** to simulate a full purchase end-to-end. Confirm the order confirmation email fires, inventory decrements, and the order appears in your admin. To enable it, go to **Settings → Payments**, deactivate your real gateway temporarily, and search for "Bogus Gateway" in the third-party providers.

*[Embed: Short Loom walkthrough video demonstrating how to enable Bogus Gateway and complete a Shopify test order]*

- **Set up abandoned cart recovery emails.** Shopify includes a built-in abandoned checkout email under **Settings → Checkout → Abandoned checkouts**, but tools like Klaviyo let you build multi-step recovery flows with better personalization and segmentation. Read our [abandoned cart recovery deep dive](/shopify-abandoned-cart-recovery).
- **Confirm trust signals at checkout.** Your checkout should display an SSL lock icon and trust badges. If you're using Shopify's hosted checkout, SSL is automatic — no additional certificate purchase needed.
- **Enable Shop Pay Installments** if your average order value is above $50. Splitting payments into four interest-free installments reduces friction on higher-ticket items and can increase average order value by up to 50% (Shopify, 2025). The tradeoff: Shopify charges a slightly higher processing fee on installment transactions, so factor that into your margin calculations.
- **Test mobile address auto-complete.** Load your checkout on your phone and verify that Google address suggestions populate correctly. A broken address field adds seconds of friction that cost you conversions.

**Real-world example:** DTC candle company Homesick reported a 17% increase in checkout completion after enabling Shop Pay Installments and adding PayPal as a backup payment option to their Shopify checkout (Shopify Plus Case Studies, 2025).

## Shipping and Tax Checklist: Protect Your Margins and Stay Compliant

Wrong shipping rates or missing tax collection will eat into your margins — or create legal problems. Most merchants who skip this section discover errors only after fulfilling dozens of mispriced orders.

- **Set up shipping zones.** At minimum, create a domestic US zone under **Settings → Shipping and Delivery**. If you sell internationally, use **Shopify Markets** to configure country-specific pricing, duties, and language settings from a single admin.
- **Choose your rate strategy.** Options include flat rate, weight-based, or carrier-calculated rates via **Shopify Shipping**. Carrier-calculated rates — where USPS, UPS, or FedEx provide real-time quotes at checkout — are available on the Shopify plan ($105/mo) and above. Flat rates are simpler to set up but risk undercharging on heavy items or overcharging on light ones, which can erode margins or increase cart abandonment.
- **Activate Shopify Tax.** Shopify's automatic US tax engine handles economic nexus rules by state, so you're collecting the right amount in every jurisdiction. Go to **Settings → Taxes and Duties** to enable it. Shopify Tax handles calculation and collection, but you're still responsible for registering with each state and remitting collected tax. Talk to a tax professional if you're unsure about your obligations.
- **Add HS codes for international products.** Harmonized System (HS) codes are standardized numerical codes that tell customs authorities what you're shipping and determine duty rates. These are required for international orders through Shopify Markets.
- **Set a free shipping threshold.** A "Free shipping on orders over $75" banner is one of the simplest ways to raise average order value. Stores using a free shipping threshold see an average AOV increase of 12% (Shopify Commerce Trends, 2026). Set your threshold slightly above your current AOV to encourage customers to add one more item.

## SEO Checklist: Get Google to Find Your Store From Day One

Organic traffic is typically the cheapest long-term traffic source. These tasks make sure Google can find, crawl, and rank your pages from day one. For a complete walkthrough, visit our [Shopify SEO guide](/shopify-seo-guide).

- **Edit page titles and meta descriptions** on every key page: homepage, collection pages, product pages, and About page. Don't leave them as Shopify defaults — generic titles like "Home" waste your most valuable on-page ranking signal.
- **Submit your sitemap to Google Search Console.** Your sitemap lives at `yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml`. Verify your site in Google Search Console and submit this URL to speed up indexing. Indexing can take anywhere from a few hours to several weeks, so submit well before launch day.
- **Connect Google Analytics 4.** Use Shopify's native GA4 integration or the **Google & YouTube** app from the Shopify App Store. Without GA4, you're blind on traffic sources and user behavior.
- **Verify structured data.** Structured data is code that helps search engines understand your page content and display rich results — like star ratings, prices, and stock status — directly in search listings. Modern Shopify themes include Product structured data by default. Test your product pages with Google's **Rich Results Test** tool to confirm price, availability, and review ratings are rendering correctly.
- **Fix duplicate content issues.** Shopify creates both `/collections/collection-name/products/product-name` and `/products/product-name` URLs for the same product. Shopify handles canonical tags automatically, but verify they're pointing to the correct version using a tool like Screaming Frog.
- **Write one blog post per major product category.** A "How to Choose the Right Running Shoe" post targeting informational keywords drives top-of-funnel traffic to your running shoe collection. Educational content builds both search authority and customer trust.
- **Clean up URL handles.** Shopify auto-generates URL slugs from your page titles. Edit them to be short and descriptive: `/products/navy-crew-tee` beats `/products/organic-cotton-crew-neck-t-shirt-navy-blue-2026-spring-collection`. Shorter URLs are easier to share, easier to read in search results, and marginally preferred by Google.
- **Check robots.txt.** Visit `yourdomain.com/robots.txt` and confirm no important pages are blocked from crawling. Shopify manages this file automatically, but it's worth verifying — especially if you've used apps that modify crawling behavior.

## Marketing and Analytics Checklist: Set Up Tracking Before Spending a Dollar

You need tracking in place *before* you spend a dollar on ads. Without it, you can't measure what's working, and optimization algorithms like Meta's Advantage+ and Google's Smart Bidding can't learn from your conversion data.

- **Install the Meta Pixel** via the **Facebook & Instagram** sales channel in Shopify. This powers retargeting ads and Advantage+ campaigns on Meta platforms. The Conversions API (CAPI), which sends server-side data alongside the browser pixel, is configured automatically through the sales channel and improves tracking accuracy as browser privacy restrictions tighten.
- **Set up Google Ads conversion tracking.** Use the Google & YouTube app to connect your store and pass purchase data back to Google Ads for Smart Bidding.
- **Build a welcome email flow.** Whether you use **Klaviyo** or **Shopify Email**, new subscribers should receive a welcome sequence within minutes of signing up. Compare the two in our [Klaviyo vs Shopify Email breakdown](/klaviyo-vs-shopify-email).
- **Connect to Google Merchant Center** for free product listings and Google Shopping ads. The Google & YouTube app syncs your product feed automatically.
- **Add an email capture form.** Use a pop-up or inline form on your homepage and blog. A 5–10% opt-in rate is a solid benchmark for new stores (Klaviyo Benchmarks, 2026). Don't trigger pop-ups immediately on page load — a 5–10 second delay or scroll-based trigger performs better and annoys fewer visitors.
- **Use UTM parameters on every paid link.** Tag your campaign URLs with `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, and `utm_campaign` so GA4 attributes revenue correctly. For example: `?utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=spring-launch`.
- **Enable Shopify Inbox** for live chat. Shoppers who chat before buying convert at 70% higher rates than those who don't (Shopify, 2026). Shopify Inbox is free and built into your admin. One caveat: live chat only works if someone is available to respond promptly. If you can't staff it consistently, set clear available hours and use automated greeting messages to manage response time expectations.

**Real-world example:** Pet supplement brand Native Pet connected their Meta Pixel and Google Ads conversion tracking before launching a $5,000 ad campaign. Because tracking was clean from day one, they identified that Meta was driving 3.2x ROAS within the first two weeks — data they would have missed entirely without pre-launch setup.

## Post-Launch Shopify Checklist: What to Do in Your First 30 Days

Launching is the starting line, not the finish. Here's what to prioritize in your first month to catch issues early and build momentum.

- **Monitor Core Web Vitals weekly** in Google Search Console. Watch for LCP (Largest Contentful Paint — how long your main content takes to load) and CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift — how much your page layout moves as it loads) regressions, especially after installing new apps.
- **Review your cart abandonment rate** in Shopify Analytics (**Analytics → Reports**). The industry average is approximately 70% (Baymard Institute, 2025). If yours is significantly higher, investigate checkout friction — common culprits include surprise shipping costs, mandatory account creation, and limited payment options.
- **Verify automated emails.** Place a real test order and confirm that order confirmation, shipping notification, and abandoned cart emails all send correctly with accurate content. Check both desktop and mobile rendering.
- **Install a heatmap tool.** Microsoft Clarity is free and shows you exactly where visitors click, scroll, and drop off. Hotjar offers similar features with session recording on a freemium plan. Heatmaps reveal behavior patterns that raw analytics numbers miss — like a "Buy Now" button that visitors scroll right past.
