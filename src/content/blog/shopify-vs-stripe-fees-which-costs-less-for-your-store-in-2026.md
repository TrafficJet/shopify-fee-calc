# Shopify vs Stripe Fees: Which Costs Less in 2026

Every dollar you lose to payment processing fees is a dollar that doesn't go toward inventory, marketing, or your next hire. Choosing between Shopify Payments and Stripe comes down to hard math — not marketing claims. This guide breaks down every fee line by line, runs real-dollar scenarios, and tells you exactly which option costs less for your specific store size.

## How Shopify and Stripe Charge Your Store

Shopify is an all-in-one e-commerce platform that includes hosting, a storefront builder, and its own built-in payment processor called Shopify Payments. Stripe is a standalone payment processor — it handles transactions but doesn't give you a store, so you integrate it into a custom site, WooCommerce build, or another platform.

This distinction matters because comparing them head-to-head isn't apples to apples. Shopify charges monthly platform fees *plus* transaction fees, while Stripe charges only per-transaction with no monthly cost.

If you run Shopify and use a third-party processor like Stripe instead of Shopify Payments, you'll pay both Stripe's fees *and* a Shopify surcharge — a detail that trips up many merchants. Both platforms take a percentage of each sale plus a flat per-transaction fee, and both tack on additional charges for international cards, chargebacks, and currency conversion. Your real cost depends on your monthly volume, average order value (AOV), and where your customers are located.

## Shopify Fee Breakdown: Plan Costs and Transaction Rates

Shopify offers three core plans, each with its own monthly price and transaction rates (Source: Shopify pricing page, 2026):

| Plan | Monthly Fee | Online Card Rate | In-Person Rate | External Processor Surcharge |
|------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|------------------------------|
| **Basic** | $39/mo | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.6% + 10¢ | 2.0% |
| **Shopify** | $105/mo | 2.6% + 30¢ | 2.5% + 10¢ | 1.0% |
| **Advanced** | $399/mo | 2.4% + 30¢ | 2.4% + 10¢ | 0.5% |

The external processor surcharge is the fee most store owners overlook. If you use any payment gateway other than Shopify Payments — including Stripe, PayPal, or Authorize.net — Shopify charges this surcharge *on top of* whatever the gateway itself charges. On the Basic plan, that's an extra 2% of every sale, which adds up fast.

**Shopify Plus** starts at approximately $2,300/mo and drops the external processor surcharge to 0.2%. Card rates through Shopify Payments are negotiable at this tier, often landing between 2.15% and 2.25% + 30¢ for online transactions (Source: Shopify Plus pricing page, 2026).

For international sales, Shopify Payments adds a 1.5% currency conversion fee when a customer pays in a currency different from your store's payout currency. Shop Pay Installments — Shopify's buy-now-pay-later option — charges the merchant up to 5.9% + 30¢ depending on the installment plan, though the customer pays no interest on qualifying orders (Source: Shopify Payments documentation, 2026).

**Real-world example:** A DTC skincare brand doing $80,000/mo in online sales on the Shopify plan pays roughly $105 + $2,320 in Shopify Payments processing fees = **$2,425/mo total**. Before upgrading from Basic, they were paying $39 + $2,560 = $2,599/mo, so the plan upgrade saved them about $174/mo — a payback period of zero months since the rate reduction immediately exceeded the higher plan cost.

## Stripe Fee Breakdown: Pay-as-You-Go Transaction Rates

Stripe keeps its pricing simpler — no mandatory monthly fee, just per-transaction charges (Source: Stripe pricing page, 2026):

| Transaction Type | Rate |
|------------------|------|
| **Online US cards (Visa, Mastercard, etc.)** | 2.9% + 30¢ |
| **In-person (Stripe Terminal)** | 2.7% + 5¢ |
| **ACH Direct Debit** | 0.8%, capped at $5 |
| **International cards** | +1.5% cross-border |
| **Currency conversion** | +1% |

The ACH Direct Debit rate is where Stripe pulls ahead for certain business models. ACH (Automated Clearing House) is a bank-to-bank transfer system — slower than card payments but dramatically cheaper. At 0.8% capped at $5 per transaction, a $2,000 B2B invoice costs just $5 to process, compared to $58.30 via standard card processing. For subscription businesses or wholesale sellers with high average order values, this alone can save thousands monthly.

Stripe Radar, the platform's built-in fraud detection tool, is included at no extra cost on the standard tier. The advanced Stripe Radar for Fraud Teams feature set — with custom rules and manual review queues — costs an additional $0.02 per screened transaction (Source: Stripe documentation, 2026).

Stripe Connect, designed for marketplace owners who split payments between multiple sellers, adds its own fee layer: 0.25% + 25¢ per payout to connected accounts on top of the standard processing rate. Merchants processing above $500,000/month can contact Stripe's sales team for custom pricing that typically drops below 2.9% (Source: Stripe documentation, 2026).

## Head-to-Head Cost Comparison: Three Real Dollar Scenarios

The only way to settle this debate is with actual numbers. Here are three scenarios using 2026 published rates.

### Scenario 1: Small Store — $10,000/mo Online Sales

| Cost Component | Shopify Basic + Shopify Payments | Stripe Only (Custom Site) | Stripe on Shopify Basic |
|----------------|----------------------------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Monthly fee | $39 | $0 | $39 |
| Processing rate | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.9% + 30¢ |
| External surcharge | $0 | $0 | 2.0% ($200) |
| Est. processing cost* | $340 | $340 | $340 |
| **Monthly total** | **$379** | **$340** | **$579** |

*Assumes 200 transactions at $50 AOV.

Stripe on its own costs $39 less per month because there's no platform fee. But you also don't get a hosted storefront, themes, or built-in cart — you'll need to build or pay for those separately. Merchants who try to replicate Shopify's feature set with a custom Stripe integration often find the development and maintenance costs exceed $39/mo within the first quarter.

### Scenario 2: Mid-Size Store — $50,000/mo Online Sales

| Cost Component | Shopify Plan + Shopify Payments | Stripe Only | Stripe on Shopify Plan |
|----------------|--------------------------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Monthly fee | $105 | $0 | $105 |
| Processing rate | 2.6% + 30¢ | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.9% + 30¢ |
| External surcharge | $0 | $0 | 1.0% ($500) |
| Est. processing cost* | $1,600 | $1,750 | $1,750 |
| **Monthly total** | **$1,705** | **$1,750** | **$2,355** |

*Assumes 1,000 transactions at $50 AOV.

At $50,000/mo, the mid-tier Shopify plan's lower rate (2.6% vs. 2.9%) saves $150/mo in processing — more than covering the $105 plan cost compared to Stripe standalone. Using Stripe *on* Shopify is the worst option by $650/mo due to the external surcharge stacking on top of Stripe's fees.

### Scenario 3: The Surcharge Trap

**Real-world example:** An outdoor gear retailer on Shopify Basic was using Stripe as their payment processor because they'd set it up before Shopify Payments was available in their region. On $30,000/mo in sales, they were paying $39 (plan) + $920 (Stripe fees) + $600 (2% surcharge) = **$1,559/mo**. After switching to Shopify Payments, their total dropped to $39 + $920 = **$959/mo** — a $600/mo savings just by removing the surcharge. They made zero changes to their pricing or marketing.

**Takeaway:** Using Stripe as an external processor on Shopify is almost always the most expensive configuration. You pay the full Stripe rate *plus* Shopify's surcharge penalty.

## Hidden Fees That Quietly Erode Your Margins

Beyond the headline transaction rates, both platforms have fees that can eat into your profits if you're not tracking them.

### Shopify Hidden Costs

- **Chargeback fee:** $15 per dispute, non-refundable even if you win the dispute (Source: Shopify documentation, 2026)
- **Premium themes:** $180–$400 one-time purchase
- **App subscriptions:** Easily total $100–$500/mo for a mid-size store running review apps, email tools, and upsell widgets
- **Currency conversion:** 1.5% on international orders paid in a different currency

### Stripe Hidden Costs

- **Chargeback fee:** $15 per dispute, but refunded if you win — a notable difference from Shopify (Source: Stripe documentation, 2026)
- **Stripe Tax:** 0.5% per transaction for automated sales tax calculation
- **Instant payouts:** 1.5% of the payout amount instead of the standard 2-business-day settlement

### The Refund Policy Difference

Stripe returns its full transaction fee when you issue a refund, following its 2024 policy update (Source: Stripe blog, 2024). Shopify Payments refunds the percentage portion but keeps the fixed 30¢ per transaction.

If your store has a 10% refund rate with 1,000 monthly transactions, that's 100 refunds × 30¢ = $30/mo you don't get back with Shopify Payments. According to the National Retail Federation, the average e-commerce return rate was 17.6% in 2023 (Source: NRF, 2024). Stores with above-average return rates — common in apparel, where Baymard Institute has documented return rates exceeding 25% — should factor this difference into their cost calculations.

## When Shopify Payments Wins on Cost

If you're already running your store on Shopify, using Shopify Payments is almost always the cheapest option. The surcharge you avoid by not using an external gateway (0.5%–2%) typically outweighs any rate advantage Stripe might offer.

The sweet spot for Shopify Payments is monthly volume between $5,000 and $150,000. In this range, upgrading your plan unlocks meaningfully lower rates — dropping from 2.9% to 2.6% or 2.4% — and the math on plan upgrades pencils out clearly. You also get consolidated billing, integrated payouts, and fewer vendor relationships to manage.

Shop Pay's accelerated checkout has been shown to increase conversion rates by up to 50% compared to guest checkout, with an average lift of 1.72 percentage points over standard checkout flows (Source: Shopify Commerce+ report, 2025). That conversion boost can translate to thousands in additional monthly revenue, which more than offsets marginal fee differences. Merchants who optimize their checkout typically find that conversion gains outweigh small processing cost savings elsewhere.

**Limitation to consider:** Shopify Payments isn't available in every country. As of 2026, it supports merchants in 23 countries (Source: Shopify documentation, 2026). If you're based outside those regions, Stripe or another third-party gateway is your only option — and the external processor surcharge is unavoidable.

## When Stripe Wins on Cost

If you're *not* on Shopify — running a headless storefront, a WooCommerce site, a custom React build, or a marketplace — Stripe is the clear winner. There's no platform surcharge eating into your margins, no monthly fee, and you only pay when you process a transaction.

Stripe's ACH Direct Debit rate (0.8%, capped at $5) is dramatically cheaper for high-ticket sales. A B2B merchant processing $100,000/mo in invoices averaging $2,500 each would pay roughly $200/mo via ACH versus $2,930/mo via standard card processing — a 93% reduction in payment costs (Source: Stripe pricing page, 2026).

**Real-world example:** A SaaS company billing enterprise clients $5,000–$20,000/mo switched from card payments to Stripe ACH and cut their annual processing costs from $42,000 to under $3,000. The tradeoff: ACH payments take 3–5 business days to settle and have a higher failure rate than cards, so this approach works best when cash flow timing isn't critical.

Stripe also wins for businesses needing usage-based billing, multi-currency treasury management, or advanced subscription logic that Shopify's ecosystem doesn't natively support.

Very high volume merchants (above $500K/mo in gross merchandise volume) can negotiate custom Stripe rates that often drop below 2.5% + 30¢, undercutting even Shopify Advanced's 2.4% + 30¢ when you factor in Shopify's $399/mo plan fee (Source: Stripe sales documentation, 2025).

**Limitation to consider:** Stripe requires more technical resources to implement and maintain than Shopify Payments. If you don't have a developer on staff or retainer, the integration and ongoing maintenance costs can offset the savings on transaction fees — particularly for stores under $50,000/mo.

## How to Calculate Your Actual Savings

Use this formula to compare your real cost between any two setups:

**Net monthly difference = (Monthly GMV × rate difference) + (plan cost difference) ± (surcharge cost)**

GMV stands for gross merchandise volume — your total sales before deductions.

Here's a worked example. Say you do $35,000/mo on Shopify Basic with Shopify Payments and you're considering whether upgrading to the Shopify plan is worth it:

- **Rate savings:** $35,000 × (2.9% − 2.6%) = $35,000 × 0.3% = **$105 saved**
- **Plan cost increase:** $105 − $39 = **$66 more per month**
- **Net savings:** $105 − $66 = **$39/mo saved by upgrading**

That $39/mo ($468/yr) comes straight back to your bottom line with no other changes. The breakeven point where the Shopify mid-tier plan pays for itself is around $22,000/mo in sales.

Pull three months of payment processor statements to find your true effective rate — the total fees paid divided by total revenue processed. This number is often higher than the published rate because of international transactions, chargebacks, and currency conversion. In Shopify's admin dashboard, navigate to **Settings > Payments > Manage** to review your projected costs at each plan tier (Source: Shopify admin documentation, 2026).

Before committing to an infrastructure change, take advantage of Shopify's free trial period and Stripe's no-commitment pricing to test both in parallel. Run a small volume of transactions through each and compare your actual statements rather than relying on published rates alone.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use Stripe as my payment processor on Shopify?
Yes, but Shopify charges an external transaction fee (0.5%–2% depending on your plan) on top of Stripe's standard rates. On most plans, this makes using Stripe on Shopify more expensive than using Shopify Payments.

### What is the cheapest way to accept payments on Shopify in 2026?
Using Shopify Payments on your current plan is almost always cheapest because you avoid the external processor surcharge. Upgrading your Shopify plan can lower your per-transaction rate enough to pay for itself if your monthly sales exceed roughly $22,000–$50,000, depending on the tier.

### Does Stripe have a monthly fee in 2026?
No. Stripe's standard plan has no monthly fee. You only pay per transaction (2.9% + 30¢ for most US card charges as of 2026). Custom pricing with lower rates is available for high-volume merchants who contact Stripe's sales team.

### Which platform has lower fees for international sales?
Both add a cross-border surcharge around 1.5%. Shopify Payments adds 1.5% for currency conversion; Stripe charges 1.5% for cross-border transactions plus an additional 1% if currency conversion is needed. For stores with significant international volume, Stripe's total international surcharge can be slightly lower in some configurations, but the difference is typically around 0.5% per transaction. Running a small batch of international test transactions through both processors is the most reliable way to compare.

### Is Shopify Plus worth it from a fee standpoint?
At roughly $2,300/month with a 0.2% external transaction fee and negotiable card rates, Shopify Plus typically starts saving money on processing costs alone around $800K–$1M+ in annual GMV, depending on your current effective rate (Source: Shopify Plus pricing page, 2026). The value proposition also includes dedicated support, advanced automation tools, and checkout customization that can improve conversion.

### Does Stripe refund transaction fees when a customer gets a refund?
As of its 2024 policy update, Stripe returns the per-transaction fee on refunds (Source: Stripe blog, 2024). Shopify Payments refunds the percentage portion but keeps the fixed 30¢ per transaction. For stores with frequent refunds — particularly in apparel or consumer electronics — this gives Stripe a small but compounding edge.
