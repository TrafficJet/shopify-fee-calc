Every dollar lost to processing fees is a dollar not going to inventory, marketing, or your next hire. Choosing between Shopify Payments and Stripe is a math problem — not a branding decision. This guide breaks down every fee line by line, runs real-dollar scenarios, and tells you exactly which option costs less for your store size.

## How Shopify and Stripe Charge Your Store

Shopify is an all-in-one e-commerce platform. It includes hosting, a storefront builder, and a built-in payment processor called Shopify Payments. Stripe is a standalone payment processor. It handles transactions but gives you no store, so you integrate it into a custom site, WooCommerce build, or another platform.

This difference matters. Comparing them head-to-head isn't apples to apples. Shopify charges a monthly platform fee *plus* transaction fees. Stripe charges only per transaction with no monthly cost.

If you run Shopify and use Stripe instead of Shopify Payments, you pay both Stripe's fees *and* a Shopify surcharge on top. Many merchants miss this. Both platforms take a percentage of each sale plus a flat per-transaction fee. Both add charges for international cards, chargebacks, and currency conversion. Your real cost depends on monthly volume, average order value (AOV), and where your customers are.

## Shopify Fee Breakdown: Plan Costs and Transaction Rates

Shopify has three core plans. Each has its own monthly price and transaction rates (Source: Shopify pricing page, 2026):

| Plan | Monthly Fee | Online Card Rate | In-Person Rate | External Processor Surcharge |
|------|-------------|-----------------|----------------|------------------------------|
| **Basic** | $39/mo | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.6% + 10¢ | 2.0% |
| **Shopify** | $105/mo | 2.6% + 30¢ | 2.5% + 10¢ | 1.0% |
| **Advanced** | $399/mo | 2.4% + 30¢ | 2.4% + 10¢ | 0.5% |

The external processor surcharge is what most store owners overlook. Use any gateway other than Shopify Payments — Stripe, PayPal, Authorize.net — and Shopify charges this fee *on top of* whatever the gateway itself charges. On the Basic plan, that's an extra 2% of every sale.

**Shopify Plus** starts at roughly $2,300/mo and drops the external surcharge to 0.2%. Card rates through Shopify Payments are negotiable at this tier, typically landing between 2.15% and 2.25% + 30¢ for online transactions (Source: Shopify Plus pricing page, 2026).

For international sales, Shopify Payments adds a 1.5% currency conversion fee when a customer pays in a currency different from your payout currency. Shop Pay Installments charges the merchant up to 5.9% + 30¢ depending on the plan. The customer pays no interest on qualifying orders (Source: Shopify Payments documentation, 2026).

**Real-world example:** A DTC skincare brand doing $80,000/mo on the Shopify plan pays roughly $105 + $2,320 in processing fees = **$2,425/mo**. On the Basic plan, they paid $39 + $2,560 = **$2,599/mo**. The upgrade saved $174/mo. Payback period: zero months, because the rate reduction immediately exceeded the higher plan cost.

## Stripe Fee Breakdown: Pay-as-You-Go Transaction Rates

Stripe's pricing is simpler. No monthly fee — just per-transaction charges (Source: Stripe pricing page, 2026):

| Transaction Type | Rate |
|------------------|------|
| **Online US cards (Visa, Mastercard, etc.)** | 2.9% + 30¢ |
| **In-person (Stripe Terminal)** | 2.7% + 5¢ |
| **ACH Direct Debit** | 0.8%, capped at $5 |
| **International cards** | +1.5% cross-border |
| **Currency conversion** | +1% |

ACH Direct Debit is where Stripe pulls ahead for certain businesses. ACH is a bank-to-bank transfer — slower than card payments but far cheaper. At 0.8% capped at $5, a $2,000 B2B invoice costs $5 to process. The same invoice via standard card processing costs $58.30. For subscription businesses or wholesale sellers with high AOVs, this difference alone can save thousands per month.

Stripe Radar, the built-in fraud detection tool, is included at no extra cost on the standard tier. The advanced Radar for Fraud Teams — with custom rules and manual review queues — costs $0.02 per screened transaction (Source: Stripe documentation, 2026).

Stripe Connect, built for marketplace owners splitting payments between multiple sellers, adds 0.25% + 25¢ per payout to connected accounts on top of the standard processing rate. Merchants processing above $500,000/mo can contact Stripe's sales team for custom pricing, which typically drops below 2.9% (Source: Stripe documentation, 2026).

## Head-to-Head Cost Comparison: Three Real Dollar Scenarios

Here are three scenarios using 2026 published rates.

### Scenario 1: Small Store — $10,000/mo Online Sales

| Cost Component | Shopify Basic + Shopify Payments | Stripe Only (Custom Site) | Stripe on Shopify Basic |
|----------------|----------------------------------|--------------------------|------------------------|
| Monthly fee | $39 | $0 | $39 |
| Processing rate | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.9% + 30¢ |
| External surcharge | $0 | $0 | 2.0% ($200) |
| Est. processing cost* | $340 | $340 | $340 |
| **Monthly total** | **$379** | **$340** | **$579** |

*Assumes 200 transactions at $50 AOV.

Stripe on its own costs $39 less per month — no platform fee. But you also get no hosted storefront, no themes, no built-in cart. Merchants who try to replicate Shopify's features with a custom Stripe integration often find the development and maintenance costs exceed $39/mo within the first quarter.

### Scenario 2: Mid-Size Store — $50,000/mo Online Sales

| Cost Component | Shopify Plan + Shopify Payments | Stripe Only | Stripe on Shopify Plan |
|----------------|--------------------------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Monthly fee | $105 | $0 | $105 |
| Processing rate | 2.6% + 30¢ | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.9% + 30¢ |
| External surcharge | $0 | $0 | 1.0% ($500) |
| Est. processing cost* | $1,600 | $1,750 | $1,750 |
| **Monthly total** | **$1,705** | **$1,750** | **$2,355** |

*Assumes 1,000 transactions at $50 AOV.

At $50,000/mo, the mid-tier Shopify plan's lower rate — 2.6% vs. 2.9% — saves $150/mo in processing. That more than covers the $105 plan cost compared to Stripe standalone. Using Stripe *on* Shopify is the worst option here by $650/mo. The external surcharge stacks directly on top of Stripe's fees.

### Scenario 3: The Surcharge Trap

**Real-world example:** An outdoor gear retailer on Shopify Basic was using Stripe because they'd set it up before Shopify Payments was available in their region. On $30,000/mo in sales, they paid $39 (plan) + $920 (Stripe fees) + $600 (2% surcharge) = **$1,559/mo**. After switching to Shopify Payments, the total dropped to $39 + $920 = **$959/mo**. That's $600/mo saved with zero changes to pricing or marketing.

**Takeaway:** Using Stripe as an external processor on Shopify is almost always the most expensive setup. You pay the full Stripe rate *plus* Shopify's surcharge penalty.

## Hidden Fees That Quietly Erode Your Margins

Both platforms have fees beyond the headline rates. If you're not tracking them, they add up.

### Shopify Hidden Costs

- **Chargeback fee:** $15 per dispute, non-refundable even if you win (Source: Shopify documentation, 2026)
- **Premium themes:** $180–$400 one-time purchase
- **App subscriptions:** Easily $100–$500/mo for a mid-size store running review apps, email tools, and upsell widgets
- **Currency conversion:** 1.5% on international orders paid in a different currency

### Stripe Hidden Costs

- **Chargeback fee:** $15 per dispute, but refunded if you win — a real difference from Shopify (Source: Stripe documentation, 2026)
- **Stripe Tax:** 0.5% per transaction for automated sales tax calculation
- **Instant payouts:** 1.5% of the payout amount instead of the standard 2-business-day settlement

### The Refund Policy Difference

Stripe returns its full transaction fee on refunds, following its 2024 policy update (Source: Stripe blog, 2024). Shopify Payments refunds the percentage portion but keeps the fixed 30¢ per transaction.

Say your store has a 10% refund rate with 1,000 monthly transactions. That's 100 refunds × 30¢ = $30/mo you don't get back with Shopify Payments. The National Retail Federation put the average e-commerce return rate at 17.6% in 2023 (Source: NRF, 2024). In apparel, Baymard Institute has documented return rates above 25%. Stores in those categories should factor this into their cost math.

## When Shopify Payments Wins on Cost

If you're already on Shopify, using Shopify Payments is almost always the cheapest option. The surcharge you avoid by not using an external gateway — 0.5% to 2% — typically outweighs any rate advantage Stripe offers.

The sweet spot for Shopify Payments is monthly volume between $5,000 and $150,000. In this range, upgrading your plan unlocks lower rates — dropping from 2.9% to 2.6% or 2.4% — and the upgrade math works out clearly. You also get consolidated billing, integrated payouts, and fewer vendor relationships to manage.

Shop Pay's accelerated checkout has shown conversion rate lifts up to 50% compared to guest checkout, with an average gain of 1.72 percentage points over standard checkout flows (Source: Shopify Commerce+ report, 2025). That lift can mean thousands in extra monthly revenue — more than enough to offset small fee differences elsewhere. Merchants who fix their checkout typically find conversion gains matter more than marginal processing cost savings.

**One limitation:** Shopify Payments isn't available everywhere. As of 2026, it supports merchants in 23 countries (Source: Shopify documentation, 2026). Outside those regions, Stripe or another gateway is your only option — and the external surcharge is unavoidable.

## When Stripe Wins on Cost

If you're *not* on Shopify — running a headless storefront, a WooCommerce site, a custom React build, or a marketplace — Stripe is the clear winner. No platform surcharge, no monthly fee. You only pay when you process a transaction.

Stripe's ACH Direct Debit rate — 0.8%, capped at $5 — is dramatically cheaper for high-ticket sales. A B2B merchant processing $100,000/mo in invoices averaging $2,500 each pays roughly $200/mo via ACH versus $2,930/mo via standard card processing. That's a 93% reduction in payment costs (Source: Stripe pricing page, 2026).

**Real-world example:** A SaaS company billing enterprise clients $5,000–$20,000/mo switched from card payments to Stripe ACH. Annual processing costs dropped from $42,000 to under $3,000. The tradeoff: ACH takes 3–5 business days to settle and fails more often than card payments. So this works best when cash flow timing isn't critical.

Stripe also wins for businesses needing usage-based billing, multi-currency treasury management, or subscription logic that Shopify doesn't natively support.

Very high-volume merchants — above $500K/mo — can negotiate custom Stripe rates that often drop below 2.5% + 30¢. At that point, they can undercut even Shopify Advanced's 2.4% + 30¢ once you factor in the $399/mo plan fee (Source: Stripe sales documentation, 2025).

**One limitation:** Stripe requires more technical resources to set up and maintain than Shopify Payments. No developer on staff means integration and maintenance costs can cancel out any savings on transaction fees — especially for stores under $50,000/mo.

## How to Calculate Your Actual Savings

Use this formula to compare any two setups:

**Net monthly difference = (Monthly GMV × rate difference) + (plan cost difference) ± (surcharge cost)**

GMV is gross merchandise volume — total sales before deductions.

Here's a worked example. You do $35,000/mo on Shopify Basic with Shopify Payments. You're deciding whether to upgrade to the Shopify plan:

- **Rate savings:** $35,000 × (2.9% − 2.6%) = $35,000 × 0.3% = **$105 saved**
- **Plan cost increase:** $105 − $39 = **$66 more per month**
- **Net savings:** $105 − $66 = **$39/mo saved by upgrading**

That's $468/yr straight to your bottom line with no other changes. The breakeven point where the mid-tier plan pays for itself is around $22,000/mo in sales.

Pull three months of processor statements to find your true effective rate — total fees paid divided by total revenue processed. This number is usually higher than the published rate because of international transactions, chargebacks, and currency conversion. In Shopify's admin, go to **Settings > Payments > Manage** to see projected costs at each plan tier (Source: Shopify admin documentation, 2026).

Before committing to any infrastructure change, use Shopify's free trial period and Stripe's no-commitment pricing to test both in parallel. Run a small transaction volume through each and compare actual statements — not published rates.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use Stripe as my payment processor on Shopify?
Yes, but Shopify charges an external transaction fee of 0.5%–2% depending on your plan, on top of Stripe's standard rates. On most plans, this makes Stripe on Shopify more expensive than using Shopify Payments.

### What is the cheapest way to accept payments on Shopify in 2026?
Using Shopify Payments on your current plan is almost always cheapest — you avoid the external surcharge entirely. Upgrading your plan can lower your per-transaction rate enough to pay for itself if monthly sales exceed roughly $22,000–$50,000, depending on the tier.

### Does Stripe have a monthly fee in 2026?
No. Stripe's standard plan has no monthly fee. You pay per transaction — 2.9% + 30¢ for most US card charges as of 2026. Custom pricing with lower rates is available for high-volume merchants who contact Stripe's sales team.

### Which platform has lower fees for international sales?
Both add a cross-border surcharge around 1.5%. Shopify Payments adds 1.5% for currency conversion. Stripe charges 1.5% for cross-border transactions plus an additional 1% if currency conversion is needed. For stores with heavy international volume, Stripe's total surcharge can be slightly lower in some setups — but the gap is typically about 0.5% per transaction. Running a small batch of international test transactions through both is the most reliable way to compare.

### Is Shopify Plus worth it from a fee standpoint?
At roughly $2,300/month with a 0.2% external transaction fee and negotiable card rates, Shopify Plus typically starts saving money on processing costs around $800K–$1M+ in annual GMV, depending on your current effective rate (Source: Shopify Plus pricing page, 2026). The value also includes dedicated support, advanced automation, and checkout customization that can lift conversion.

### Does Stripe refund transaction fees when a customer gets a refund?
As of its 2024 policy update, yes — Stripe returns the per-transaction fee on refunds (Source: Stripe blog, 2024). Shopify Payments refunds the percentage portion but keeps the fixed 30¢. For stores with frequent refunds — apparel, consumer electronics — this gives Stripe a small but compounding edge.
