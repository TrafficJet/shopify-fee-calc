# How to Set Up Shopify Payments (2026 Guide)

*We tested this entire setup process on a live US Shopify store in January 2026. Every step and screenshot reference below reflects the current Shopify admin interface.*

Shopify Payments is one of the first things you should configure when launching your store. It cuts extra transaction fees, gets money into your account faster, and keeps your entire payment workflow in one dashboard. This guide covers activation, configuration, fees, and troubleshooting — all updated for 2026.

---

## What Shopify Payments Is and Why It Saves You Money

Shopify Payments is Shopify's built-in payment processor. It runs on Stripe on the backend. Instead of connecting a separate payment gateway — a third-party service that authorizes and processes card transactions — you activate Shopify Payments directly from your admin panel. Then you start accepting credit cards, debit cards, and digital wallets right away.

The biggest benefit: no third-party transaction fee. When you use external processors, Shopify charges 0.5% to 2% per transaction depending on your plan. That fee disappears entirely with Shopify Payments (Shopify, 2026). On the Basic plan, a store doing $10,000 per month saves up to $200 every month.

Shopify Payments also unlocks Shop Pay. This is the accelerated checkout that stores customer shipping and payment details for faster purchases. Shop Pay has been shown to increase checkout conversion by up to 50% compared to guest checkout (Shopify, 2025). As of 2026, Shopify Payments is available in 23 countries — the US, Canada, UK, Australia, Germany, Japan, and others.

**How does it compare to alternatives?** PayPal charges 2.99% + $0.49 per transaction, and the Shopify third-party fee stacks on top. Standalone Stripe has similar card rates but still triggers that extra Shopify fee. Square works well for in-person retail but lacks the deep Shopify integration that keeps everything in one place.

**Real-world example:** Brooklyn-based candle brand Otherland switched from PayPal to Shopify Payments and reportedly cut payment processing costs by over 15%. They also gained access to Shop Pay's one-tap checkout.

## Shopify Payments Requirements and Eligibility

Before you activate Shopify Payments, confirm your store meets these requirements.

**Supported countries (as of 2026):** United States, Canada, United Kingdom, Australia, New Zealand, Ireland, Germany, Austria, Belgium, the Netherlands, Denmark, Sweden, Finland, Italy, Spain, France, Portugal, Singapore, Hong Kong, Japan, Czech Republic, Romania, and Switzerland (Shopify, 2026).

**Business documentation:** You need a valid Social Security Number (SSN) or Employer Identification Number (EIN) for US stores, plus a bank account in the same supported country. That bank account must accept ACH transfers — electronic bank-to-bank deposits. Wire transfer-only accounts won't work.

**Restricted business types:** Shopify Payments does not support high-risk products or services. This includes CBD, firearms, ammunition, adult content, cryptocurrency, and multi-level marketing. Review Shopify's full Acceptable Use Policy before applying.

Shopify Payments is available on every paid plan: Basic ($39/month), Shopify ($105/month), Advanced ($399/month), and Plus (starting at $2,300/month) as of 2026 (Shopify, 2026). The account holder must be at least 18 years old.

Merchants who skip the eligibility check sometimes discover mid-setup that their product category is restricted. That can mean account suspension after orders are already flowing. Check your product type against the Acceptable Use Policy before you begin.

## Step-by-Step: Activate Shopify Payments in About 10 Minutes

Follow these eight steps to get Shopify Payments running on your store.

**Step 1 — Open your payment settings.** Log in to your Shopify admin and go to **Settings > Payments**. You'll see a section labeled "Shopify Payments" at the top of the page.

**Step 2 — Start account setup.** Click **"Complete account setup"** under the Shopify Payments card. If you had a third-party gateway active before, Shopify will ask whether to replace it or keep it as a secondary option.

**Step 3 — Select your business type.** Choose from Individual/Sole Proprietor, LLC, Partnership, or Corporation. This determines what tax documentation Shopify requests.

**Step 4 — Enter personal details.** Provide your legal first and last name as it appears on government ID, your date of birth, and the last four digits of your SSN. If your business is an LLC or corporation, also enter the full EIN.

**Step 5 — Add your business address and phone number.** Use the physical address where your business operates — not a P.O. box. Shopify uses this for identity verification and tax reporting.

**Step 6 — Connect your US bank account.** Enter your bank's routing number and account number. Double-check that the routing number is for ACH transfers, not wire transfers. These are often different numbers at major banks like Chase and Bank of America. You can also connect a Shopify Balance account here for faster access to funds.

**Step 7 — Enable two-step authentication.** Shopify requires this for all Shopify Payments accounts. Set it up via SMS, an authenticator app, or a hardware security key under **Settings > Users and permissions**.

**Step 8 — Save and verify.** Click **Save** to submit your information. Most accounts are verified instantly, and you can begin processing payments right away. If Shopify needs more documentation — a photo ID or business license — you'll see a banner in your admin with upload instructions. Pending verifications typically resolve within 1–3 business days.

**Important:** While verification is pending, Shopify may hold payouts. You can still accept orders, but funds won't transfer to your bank until verification is complete.

## Configure Payout Settings to Match Your Cash Flow Needs

Once Shopify Payments is active, you control when and how you receive your money.

Go to **Settings > Payments > Manage > Payout schedule** to choose between daily, weekly, or monthly payouts. The US default is daily payouts with a 2-business-day rolling window. A sale on Monday typically hits your bank by Wednesday (Shopify, 2026).

Weekly payouts let you pick a specific day, which can simplify bookkeeping. Monthly payouts deposit on a set date each month. You can also set a minimum payout threshold so small amounts don't trigger individual bank transfers.

Want even faster access to revenue? Use Shopify Balance instead of a traditional bank account. With daily payouts enabled, that means next-business-day access with no extra transfer delays. There are no monthly fees for Shopify Balance as of 2026 (Shopify, 2026).

One limitation worth knowing: Shopify Balance is not a standard bank account and is not FDIC-insured in the traditional sense. Funds are held by Shopify's banking partner. The account also lacks some features of a full business checking account — no check writing, and not all accounting platforms integrate with it. For most merchants, it works best as a complement to a primary business bank account, not a replacement.

**Real-world example:** A US-based pet supply store switched from weekly to daily payouts after a product went viral on TikTok. Daily payouts let them reinvest revenue into restocking inventory within 48 hours, rather than waiting for Friday's lump-sum deposit.

## Accepted Payment Methods and Currencies

Shopify Payments accepts all major credit and debit cards: **Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, JCB, and Diners Club**.

For digital wallets, you can enable **Shop Pay, Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Meta Pay** directly from your Shopify Payments settings. Shop Pay is especially valuable because it stores customer information for repeat one-tap purchases. It's also exclusive to stores using Shopify Payments.

If you want buy now, pay later (BNPL), **Shop Pay Installments** — powered by Affirm — lets customers split purchases into biweekly or monthly payments. You receive the full order amount upfront. Affirm handles the customer's installment plan (Shopify, 2026). Offering BNPL options can increase average order value by 50% or more, according to Affirm's merchant data (Affirm, 2025). Results vary by product category and price point.

For international selling, enable **multi-currency presentment** under **Settings > Markets**. Customers browse and pay in their local currency — euros, pounds, yen, and dozens more. Your payouts still arrive in USD for US-based stores. Shopify applies a **1.5% currency conversion fee** on top of the standard card processing rate (Shopify, 2026). That adds up fast for stores with high international volume, so factor it into your pricing.

## Shopify Payments Fees: What You'll Actually Pay per Transaction

Shopify Payments has no monthly fee beyond your Shopify plan cost. You pay per transaction based on your plan tier.

| Plan | Online Credit Card Rate | In-Person (POS) Rate | Third-Party Fee |
|------|------------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Basic ($39/mo) | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.6% + 10¢ | 2.0% (waived with Shopify Payments) |
| Shopify ($105/mo) | 2.6% + 30¢ | 2.5% + 10¢ | 1.0% (waived with Shopify Payments) |
| Advanced ($399/mo) | 2.4% + 30¢ | 2.4% + 10¢ | 0.6% (waived with Shopify Payments) |
| Plus ($2,300+/mo) | Negotiable | Negotiable | 0.2% (waived with Shopify Payments) |

*(As of 2026. Source: Shopify)*

The **chargeback fee is $15 per dispute**. Win the dispute, and Shopify refunds that $15. No other processor on Shopify's platform offers a refundable chargeback fee. That's a real advantage for stores in categories with higher dispute rates.

Using a third-party gateway like PayPal or Authorize.net means paying their processing fee plus Shopify's transaction surcharge on top. On the Basic plan, a store processing $50,000 per year through PayPal pays an extra $1,000 in fees that Shopify Payments would eliminate entirely.

One trade-off: Shopify Payments rates are fixed per plan tier with no volume discounts below Plus. Merchants processing over $1 million annually on the Advanced plan might find that negotiating custom rates with a standalone processor like Stripe or Adyen — even after the third-party fee — can sometimes cost less in total. But for the vast majority of small and mid-size stores, Shopify Payments is the most cost-effective option.

## How to Manage Chargebacks and Reduce Dispute Losses

Chargebacks happen when a customer contacts their bank to reverse a charge. Find and manage disputes under **Finances > Chargebacks** — or **Orders > Disputes** in some admin versions.

When a chargeback is filed, you typically have **7 to 21 days to respond**, depending on the card network. Visa and Mastercard tend to allow more time than American Express. Miss the deadline and you automatically lose the dispute — plus forfeit the transaction amount and the $15 fee.

**Evidence to submit with your response:**

- Order confirmation and receipt
- Shipping confirmation with tracking number and delivery proof
- Customer communication (emails, chat logs)
- Screenshots of your refund policy and product description
- AVS (Address Verification Service) match results — AVS compares the billing address the customer entered with what the card issuer has on file

Shopify's built-in fraud analysis flags suspicious orders before they become chargebacks. For orders shipped with Shopify Shipping that have valid tracking, Shopify also offers an **automated chargeback rebuttal tool** that compiles evidence and submits it on your behalf (Shopify, 2026).

**Prevention tips:** Write clear product descriptions and use accurate photos. Always provide tracking numbers. Respond to customer support inquiries within 24 hours. Stores with responsive customer service see up to 40% fewer chargebacks, according to Chargebacks911's 2025 industry report.

**Real-world example:** A US electronics accessories store cut their chargeback rate from 1.2% to 0.3% by adding delivery photo confirmation through their shipping carrier and putting their return policy on every product page. The industry average is roughly 0.60% according to Midigator's 2024 benchmark data — so their starting rate of 1.2% was already a red flag.

## Common Setup Problems and How to Fix Them

**"Shopify Payments is not available in your region."** Your store's registered country isn't among the 23 supported countries. Check your store address under **Settings > Store details**. If your country genuinely isn't supported, you'll need a third-party gateway like PayPal or standalone Stripe.

**Verification stuck or rejected.** The most common cause is a mismatch between your legal name, SSN/EIN, and what the IRS has on file. Re-enter your details carefully against your tax documents. If Shopify requests an ID document, upload a clear, high-resolution photo of a government-issued ID. Blurry or cropped images are the top reason for delays.

**Payouts on hold.** Shopify may hold payouts if your store has an unusually high refund rate, a spike in chargebacks, or a sudden surge in order volume that looks suspicious. Check your email for messages from Shopify's risk team and respond with the requested documentation quickly. Merchants who have been through this report that providing invoices, supplier documentation, and proof of advertising spend typically resolves holds within 3–5 business days.

**Bank account not accepted.** Confirm your routing number is for ACH transfers — not the wire transfer routing number. These are different at many banks, including Chase and Bank of America. Check your bank's website or call to verify. Your account must also be a checking account. Some savings accounts don't support ACH deposits.

**Need help?** Contact Shopify Support through **Settings > Support** in your admin dashboard. Live chat, email, and phone callback are available 24/7 on all paid plans (Shopify, 2026).

## Shopify Payments vs. Third-Party Processors: Side-by-Side Comparison

Here's how Shopify Payments stacks up against the most popular alternatives for US-based Shopify stores:

| Feature | Shopify Payments | PayPal | Stripe (Standalone) | Square |
|---------|-----------------|--------|---------------------|--------|
| Online rate (Basic plan) | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.99% + 49¢ | 2.9% + 30¢ | 2.9% + 30¢ |
| Shopify third-party fee | None | 2.0% | 2.0% | 2.0% |
| Shop Pay access | Yes | No | No | No |
| Built-in fraud detection | Yes | Yes | Yes (Radar) | Yes |
| PCI compliance managed | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Payout speed (US) | 2 business days | Instant to PayPal balance | 2 business days | 1–2 business days |

*(Sources: Shopify, PayPal, Stripe, Square — as of 2026)*

The biggest differentiator is the **elimination of Shopify's third-party transaction fee**. Using PayPal or standalone Stripe on a Basic plan means paying their processing fee plus Shopify's 2% surcharge. That makes each transaction cost close to 5% in total.

**When to add PayPal alongside Shopify Payments:** Some customers still prefer PayPal — especially international buyers. According to Baymard Institute's 2024 checkout usability research, roughly 30% of US online shoppers used PayPal in the past year. Adding PayPal as a secondary option gives those customers a familiar checkout without giving up your Shopify Payments benefits on other orders.

Shopify Payments includes built-in fraud analysis. It flags risky orders based on signals like mismatched billing addresses and failed CVV checks. To get similar protection with other processors, you'd need Stripe Radar — free with Stripe — or a third-party fraud app.

Shopify Payments is PCI DSS Level 1 compliant, which is the highest certification level in the payment card industry. You don't touch any compliance paperwork yourself (Shopify, 2026). That alone saves hours of administrative work that standalone gateway users deal with every year.

For most US Shopify stores, Shopify Payments is the clear default. It's typically cheaper, fully integrated, and gives you Shop Pay and Shopify Balance — two features no third-party processor can offer. The main cases where a different processor makes more sense: your product category is restricted, your store is in an unsupported country, or your volume is high enough to negotiate rates that beat Shopify's fixed tiers even after the third-party fee.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I use Shopify Payments on the free trial?
You can set up Shopify Payments during a free trial, but you cannot process live transactions until you select a paid Shopify plan.

### How long does Shopify Payments verification take?
Most accounts are verified instantly. In some cases, Shopify may request additional documents, which typically take 1–3 business days to review.

### Does Shopify Payments work with Shopify POS?
Yes. Shopify Payments is the default processor for Shopify POS in the US. You'll need a Shopify card reader (such as the Tap & Chip Reader or the POS Terminal) to accept in-person payments.

### What happens to my funds if Shopify suspends my Payments account?
Shopify may hold funds for up to 120 days during a review. Contact Shopify Support immediately and provide any requested business documentation to speed up the process. Merchants who respond within 48 hours with complete documentation generally see faster resolutions.

### Can I accept international payments with Shopify Payments?
Yes. Shopify Payments supports multiple presentment currencies. Customers see prices in their local currency, and you receive payouts in USD (for US stores), minus a 1.5% conversion fee per transaction.

### Is Shopify Payments PCI compliant?
Yes. Shopify Payments is PCI DSS Level 1 compliant — the highest certification level in the payment card industry. You do not need to handle PCI compliance separately; Shopify manages it entirely.
